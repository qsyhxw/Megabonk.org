Steam Leaderboards (Official)

The official Megabonk leaderboards are integrated directly into the game on Steam and maintained by the developer (vedinad). These rankings are automatically recorded from your in-game runs.

‚úÖ Official Platform: Access through the game itself on Steam. Rankings update automatically when you complete runs.

Key Features of Steam Leaderboards

üéÆ Automatic Submission

Your scores are automatically submitted when you complete a run. No manual video upload required.

üîí Developer-Maintained

The developer actively monitors for cheating, issues bans, and maintains system integrity.

üîÑ Patch-Based Resets

Leaderboards reset when major balance changes make previous scores non-comparable (e.g., v1.0.7, v1.0.12).

‚úÖ Anti-Cheat Protection

Built-in detection system flags suspicious scores. Developer reviews and bans confirmed cheaters.

Major Leaderboard Updates

  • v1.0.7 (Sept 29, 2025): Leaderboard reset due to cheating issues affecting system stability
  • v1.0.12 (Oct 8, 2025): "Leaderboard fix and Balancing" - Fixed vulnerabilities, cleared illegitimate scores, strengthened anti-cheat. Also reworked Final Swarm to prevent infinite farming exploits
  • Ongoing: Developer continues to monitor and ban confirmed cheaters

üìä What Gets Tracked

The primary competitive metric is Total Kill Count in a single run. Rankings are organized by character and game version/patch.

megabonk.fun (Community VOD Leaderboard)

megabonk.fun is a community-operated leaderboard that requires full video proof for all submissions. Created and maintained by community members, it operates independently from the developer.

üîπ Not Official: megabonk.fun explicitly states "Not affiliated with vedinad" (the game developer). This is a community project with its own submission rules and verification process.

megabonk.fun Features

üìπ VOD-Verified

All submissions require full-run video evidence from start to finish.

üë• Community Moderated

Volunteer moderators review submitted videos and verify legitimacy.

üéØ One Per Character

Submit one high score per character. With 20 playable characters, you can have multiple leaderboard entries.

üìú Version Archives

Past game patches are archived and viewable via filters, preserving historical records.

megabonk.fun Submission Requirements

  1. Full Run Video: Must show the entire run from start to finish (no editing)
  2. Clear Score Screenshot: Final score screen must be clearly visible
  3. Current Patch: Run must be on the latest game version
  4. No Exploits: No mods, hacks, exploits, or unauthorized tools
  5. Video Platform: Upload to YouTube or similar platform and submit the link
  6. Player Name: Provide your display name with the submission

Review Times (Community Board)

According to megabonk.fun, submissions during working hours (EST timezone) are typically reviewed within 1 hour. Outside those hours, expect delays of several hours to a full day, as this is run by volunteers.

Recent Updates (megabonk.fun v0.4.0, Nov 2025):

  • Previous game patches now archived as "static" viewable records
  • Enhanced filtering options for viewing by character/version
  • Weekly reset schedule adjusted (Sundays, 4 hours earlier for moderator convenience)

Understanding Leaderboard Resets

Both leaderboard systems reset when major game updates make previous scores incomparable. This happens when:

  • Characters receive significant balance changes
  • Core game mechanics are fundamentally altered
  • New content changes scoring potential (e.g., Final Swarm rework in v1.0.12)
  • Exploits are patched that invalidate previous strategies

What happens to old scores?

Both systems archive previous patch data. Steam maintains version history, while megabonk.fun makes old patches "static" and filterable, preserving historical achievements while maintaining competitive integrity.

Playable Characters

As of the latest Steam store description, Megabonk features 20 playable characters, each with unique abilities and playstyles. Both leaderboard systems organize rankings by character, allowing specialization and competition across multiple categories.

Competitive Tips

  • Focus on One System: Decide if you want official Steam rankings or community VOD validation
  • Record Your Runs: Even for Steam, recording helps you analyze gameplay and proves legitimacy
  • Study Top Strategies: Watch leaderboard leaders to learn optimal builds and techniques
  • Stay Updated: Follow patch notes for meta shifts and balance changes
  • Master One Character: Specialize before branching out to multiple characters
  • Understand Final Swarm: Reaching and surviving Final Swarm consistently is crucial for high kill counts

Common Questions

‚ùì Which leaderboard is "official"?

The Steam in-game leaderboards are the official rankings maintained by the developer. megabonk.fun is a valuable community resource but is not affiliated with the developer.

‚ùì Can I submit to both leaderboards?

Yes! Your runs automatically appear on Steam leaderboards. If you also record full video, you can submit the same run to megabonk.fun for community verification.

‚ùì Why was my Steam score flagged or removed?

The developer's anti-cheat system may flag:

  • Statistical anomalies or impossible values
  • Signs of memory tampering or exploits
  • Scores from outdated game versions
  • Patterns consistent with known cheats

Confirmed cheaters receive bans from the developer.

‚ùì How do I access Steam leaderboards?

Open Megabonk on Steam and navigate to the leaderboards section within the game. You can also view them through the Steam Community pages for the game.

‚ùì Are there regional restrictions?

No. Both Steam leaderboards and megabonk.fun are globally accessible. Players from all countries can view and compete.

‚ùì What about megabonk.gg?

megabonk.gg is another community leaderboard site that tracks weekly rankings. Like megabonk.fun, it's community-run and not affiliated with the developer. Multiple community boards can coexist.
