üõ°Ô∏è How Slippery Ring Works

Slippery Ring is a default item that provides a simple yet effective defensive layer: +15% to your internal Evasion stat. When you evade an attack, you take no damage at all and the game does not register you as being "hit."

Evasion Mechanics Explained

Understanding how evasion works is critical to maximizing Slippery Ring's value:

  • Chance-Based Defense: Each incoming attack triggers an evasion roll. Your evasion percentage is your chance to completely dodge that attack
  • No Hit Status: When you evade, the game treats it as if you were never touched. This prevents speed loss, maintains buffs, and keeps you untouchable
  • Hyperbolic Scaling: Evasion uses the formula Effective Evasion = Internal Value / (1 + Internal Value), meaning diminishing returns kick in as you stack more evasion
  • Soft Cap at ~70%: Due to the scaling formula, effective evasion practically caps around 70%, even with extremely high internal values
üìä Diminishing Returns Example

When you have 35% existing evasion and pick up Slippery Ring, it will only add approximately 5% actual evasion (not the full 15% stated). This is because evasion has built-in diminishing returns that become more pronounced at higher values.

Source: Community testing via Steam Discussions

Why Slippery Ring is Good

Despite the diminishing returns, Slippery Ring remains one of the best defensive items:

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much evasion does Slippery Ring actually provide?
Short Answer: +15% to internal evasion stat, but actual effective evasion gain varies based on your existing evasion due to diminishing returns.

Example: At 0% base evasion, Slippery Ring adds ~13% effective. At 35% base evasion, it only adds ~5% effective.

Q: Can you stack multiple Slippery Rings?
Yes, Slippery Rings are stackable. However, due to evasion's hyperbolic scaling formula, stacking more than 3-4 rings provides minimal benefit. The community recommends stopping at 2-3 rings and diversifying your defense instead.
Q: Is there an evasion cap?
Soft cap at ~70%. While there's no hard cap, evasion's diminishing returns formula makes it practically impossible to exceed 70% effective evasion, even with extremely high internal values. Some players report reaching 82-95% in extreme cases, but these are outliers.
Q: Does Slippery Ring work against bosses?
Yes, but with exceptions. Evasion works against most boss attacks. However, some boss abilities or mechanics may bypass evasion entirely. Additionally, relying solely on RNG-based evasion against bosses is risky - always have backup defense layers.
Q: Should I prioritize Slippery Ring or Evasion Tome?
Both are excellent. Slippery Ring provides an immediate +15% boost (great early game), while Evasion Tome scales with levels. For Ninja builds, prioritize Evasion Tome early, then add 2-3 Slippery Rings mid-game. For other builds, 1-2 rings is sufficient.
Q: Is Slippery Ring good for beginners?
Absolutely. Slippery Ring is one of the best beginner items because it:
  • Has no unlock requirement
  • Provides pure defense with no downsides
  • Works universally across all characters
  • Teaches the value of evasion mechanics
Q: Does Luck affect evasion?
No. Luck only affects item drops and critical hit chance. It does not influence evasion rolls or any defensive mechanics. This has been confirmed by multiple community tests.
Q: What's the best character to use Slippery Ring with?
Ninja is the absolute best due to his execution passive and natural evasion scaling. Calcium is a close second for Speed Demon builds that cannot afford to get hit. However, Slippery Ring is viable on every character.
