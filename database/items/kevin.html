üß™ How Kevin Works

Kevin is one of the most unique items in Megabonk. Instead of directly buffing your character, it creates a controlled self-damage loop that can be weaponized with the right build.

Mechanics Breakdown

  • Self-Damage Trigger: Approximately 25% chance per hit to damage yourself
  • Cannot Kill: Kevin will never deliver a killing blow - it stops at 1 HP
  • Bypasses Shields: Deals damage directly to your health pool, ignoring all Shield Tome effects
  • Triggers "When Hit" Effects: Each self-punch counts as you taking damage, reliably triggering items that activate when you're hit, such as Mirror, Electric Plug, Toxic Barrel, and Spiky Shield
  • Scales with Attack Speed: More hits = more Kevin procs = more on-hit triggers (pairs well with Agility Tome)
üî¨ Technical Detail

Kevin's self-damage always stops at 1 HP and cannot kill you. Community testing suggests that stacking Armor Tome doesn't fully negate Kevin's self-damage, but the exact interaction isn't documented in official patch notes. Treat Kevin as a tool to safely stay at low HP rather than a source of "free" tankiness.

Why Would You Use This?

The key insight: Many powerful items trigger when YOU get hit, not when you hit enemies. Kevin lets you proc these effects on demand, turning defensive items into offensive weapons.

‚úì Advantages

  • Enables on-hit synergies with normally defensive items
  • Cannot kill you - provides a safety floor at 1 HP
  • Excellent with lifesteal and regen builds
  • Creates unique build archetypes impossible without it
  • Bypassing shields means you can stay at low HP intentionally for Demonic Soul synergy

‚úó Disadvantages

  • Constantly keeps you at dangerously low HP
  • Requires strong sustain to avoid one-shot deaths
  • Useless without synergy items
  • Makes Golden Shield and shield builds nearly worthless
  • High skill floor - easy to die if not prepared

üîì How to Unlock Kevin

Unlock Requirement

Obtain 3 Leeching Crystal items in a single run.

  1. Level Regen Tome to Level 8
    Leeching Crystal only appears in your item pool once Regen Tome reaches Level 8. This is a prerequisite.
  2. Start a New Run with High Sustain
    Play a character with good regen or lifesteal (like Megachad, Athena, or Calcium).
  3. Hunt for Leeching Crystals
    Open every chest. Leeching Crystal is rare but appears more frequently once Regen Tome is leveled. Focus on Luck Tome to increase chest quality.
  4. Collect All Three in One Run
    You must get all 3 Leeching Crystals in the same playthrough. Progress resets if you die or complete the run.
  5. Kevin Unlocks Permanently
    Once unlocked, Kevin joins your item pool and can appear in all future runs.
üí° Pro Tip: Fast Unlock Strategy

Disable non-essential items in your pool to increase Leeching Crystal spawn rates. Play quick Tier 1 runs and reset early if you don't see crystals by Stage 2. The Forest map is recommended for fast cycling.

What is Leeching Crystal?

Effect (subject to patch changes): Increases Max HP significantly while cutting your HP Regen by 50%.

‚ö†Ô∏è Version Note: Different community guides list this as either +50 Max HP or +50% Max HP with -50% HP Regen. Always double-check the in-game tooltip for the current patch and update your build math accordingly.

Leeching Crystal trades regen for raw HP. It's a double-edged sword that synergizes with max HP scaling items like Beefy Ring (+20% damage per 100 Max HP) and Health Tome.

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can Kevin kill you?
No. Kevin can bring you to 1 HP, but it will never deliver the killing blow. However, this means you're extremely vulnerable to any enemy damage while at low HP.
Q: Why can't I find Leeching Crystal?
Leeching Crystal only appears after Regen Tome reaches Level 8. If your Regen Tome isn't high enough level, the crystal won't spawn in your item pool. Focus on leveling Regen Tome first by picking it whenever available.
Q: Do Kevin procs ignore shields?
Yes. Kevin's self-damage bypasses all shield layers (including Golden Shield, Aegis, and Shield Tome) and deals damage directly to your health pool.
Q: Is Kevin good for beginners?
No. Kevin is one of the hardest items to use effectively. It requires specific synergy items, strong sustain, and game knowledge to avoid dying. New players should unlock it for completion but avoid using it until comfortable with the game. Practice with easier builds first, like Bullet Hell or Sword Master.
Q: What's the best character for Kevin?
Megachad is the best Kevin character due to his high base HP and sustain capabilities. Athena and Calcium are also good choices. Avoid glass cannon characters like Ninja.
Q: Does Kevin work with all on-hit items?
Kevin triggers items that proc when YOU get hit, not when you hit enemies. Items like Mirror, Electric Plug, Thunder Mitts, Toxic Barrel, and Spiky Shield all work. Items that require you to hit enemies (like crit effects) do not synergize.
Q: Can I stack multiple Kevins?
Technically yes ‚Äì Kevin isn't hard-limited to one copy ‚Äì but stacking several usually isn't worth it. Most meta builds pick a single Kevin and focus on multiplying on-hit trigger items (Mirror, Electric Plug, Toxic Barrel, etc.) instead.
Q: Is the "Kevin Immortality Build" still viable?
The original immortality exploit was patched in v1.0.12+. However, Kevin + Mirror builds remain viable - they're just no longer game-breaking. See our updated Kevin Build Guide for current strategies that work in 2025.
Q: What weapons work best with Kevin?
High attack speed weapons maximize Kevin procs. Best choices: Aura (continuous hits), Lightning Staff (chain lightning), Bow (with pierce), and Katana (fast melee). Pair with Agility Tome for maximum effect.
Q: How does Kevin interact with Thorns builds?
Kevin has excellent synergy with Thorns Tome and thorn-based items. Each Kevin self-hit can trigger thorns effects, essentially doubling your thorn damage output. Combine with Mirror for maximum reflect damage.
