üìã Overview

Version 1.0.12 represents a major stability and balance update for Megabonk, addressing critical issues that emerged during the game's explosive first month. Following leaderboard exploits that disrupted the competitive community and meta strategies that resulted in multi-hour endgame sessions, developer vedinad implemented comprehensive fixes alongside targeted weapon adjustments.

This patch focuses on three core areas: securing leaderboard integrity through anti-cheat measures and permanent ban systems, redesigning the Final Swarm phase to create faster and more challenging endgame experiences, and rebalancing progression systems to encourage build diversity rather than single-strategy dominance.

üëª Final Swarm Rework

The endgame phase received a complete overhaul to address the problematic meta that encouraged players to endure hours-long sessions at sub-2 FPS framerates. The previous Final Swarm could be indefinitely sustained through execution chains and Black Hole stalling, creating an unhealthy competitive environment where leaderboard positions were determined by endurance rather than skill.

Developer Commentary: "The current meta required you to wear sunglasses and watch a slideshow at 1.5fps for 5 hours to get a WR. This was never intended, and I don't think anyone actually enjoyed that."

Key Changes

  • New Ghost-Type Enemies ‚Äì Special enemy variants spawn during Final Swarm that are significantly harder to control
  • Reduced Control Effectiveness ‚Äì Execution mechanics and Black Hole crowd control are substantially less effective, forcing active movement and adaptation
  • Faster Escalation ‚Äì The swarm now reaches lethal density more quickly, creating natural run endings between 20-45 minutes instead of 3-5 hours
  • Skill-Based Survival ‚Äì Success now depends on positioning, resource management, and build optimization rather than patience

Meta Impact: The update shifts competitive strategy from "infinite sustain + stall" to "burst damage + efficient routing." Builds focused on raw DPS and mobility are now favored over pure survivability compositions. Check out our updated build guides for post-patch strategies.

‚öîÔ∏è Detailed Weapon Analysis

Black Hole

Role Change: Crowd Control ‚Üí Crowd Control (Reduced Synergy)

  • Size Buff: 20% larger base projectile improves baseline effectiveness without item investment
  • Proc Nerf: Coefficient reduced from 0.9 to 0.7, matching other AoE weapons
  • Impact: Still excellent for pure crowd control, but less explosive when combined with on-hit items like Moldy Cheese. Remains viable but no longer dominates late-game compositions. See optimal builds in our Black Hole Build Guide.

Joe's Dagger

Scaling Cap Introduced: 200% damage increase per minute per dagger

  • Example: With 10 daggers equipped, maximum scaling is 2000% damage per minute (previously unlimited)
  • Timeline: At 10 daggers, reaching damage cap takes 10 minutes instead of scaling infinitely
  • Developer Note: "Before this change, you could get 20,000% damage in 10 seconds, which nothing else in the game could contest lol"
  • Impact: Remains a strong carry weapon through early and mid-game. No longer the sole viable option for record-breaking marathon runs. Encourages weapon diversity at high levels

Chaos Tome

Risk/Reward Rebalanced: 50% increase to all stat bonuses

  • Previous State: High-risk gamble with underwhelming random stat gains
  • New State: All random stat bonuses are 50% higher, making favorable RNG significantly more impactful
  • Best Use: Experimental builds with high XP gain for frequent rerolls; avoid on highest difficulties where consistency is critical
  • Community Reception: Now considered a viable alternative to meta Tome selections. Check our Tome Tier List for updated rankings.

XP Tome

Progression Speed Reduced: 9% ‚Üí 7% XP gain per level

  • Justification: The majority of competitive players requested this nerf to reduce early-game power spike disparity
  • Effect: Slower leveling means delayed power spikes and more deliberate early-game planning
  • Developer Stance: "At least give it a try, and If everyone ends up hating it, I can just revert it back to 9% next week. please go easy on me"
  • Current Status: Change is provisional pending community feedback

Bush (Marksman Passive)

Scaling Improved: Cooldown reduction now provides clear milestones

  • Starting Cooldown: 5 seconds
  • Scaling Rate: -0.4 seconds every 10 levels
  • Level 100 Cooldown: 1 second (cap)
  • Breakpoints: Level 10 (4.6s), Level 50 (3s), Level 75 (2s), Level 100 (1s)
  • Impact: More predictable power scaling makes Bush a stronger pick for execution-focused builds. See our Bush Best Build guide for optimal strategies.
