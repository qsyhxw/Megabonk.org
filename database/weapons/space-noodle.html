Space Noodle makes attached enemies completely invulnerable. This counterintuitive mechanic transforms dangerous enemies into temporary immortal threats, directly contradicting survival gameplay. Most experienced players immediately toggle this weapon off to prevent it from appearing in runs.

üìã Weapon Overview

Space Noodle is a duration-based beam weapon that automatically attaches to the nearest enemy, creating a green energy beam between player and target. Any enemies passing through the beam take damage, but the attached enemy becomes completely invulnerable to all damage sources for the entire duration.

This creates catastrophic scenarios where:

  • Dangerous elite enemies become unkillable while chasing you
  • Final Swarm ghosts remain invulnerable and continue dealing damage
  • Natural kiting and survival strategies are disrupted
  • You must adopt awkward positioning to funnel enemies through the beam

Despite one outlier S-tier rating from PCGamesN (likely an error), overwhelming community consensus, player testing, and competitive analysis confirm Space Noodle as one of Megabonk's worst weapons.

‚öôÔ∏è Core Mechanics

How It Works:

  • Auto-targeting: Beam attaches to nearest enemy (cannot be manually controlled)
  • Beam damage: 10 base damage per tick to enemies crossing the beam
  • Invulnerability: Attached enemy is immune to ALL damage sources
  • Duration-based: Beam persists for set time before resetting
  • Forced positioning: Must position enemies between yourself and tethered target

The Fundamental Flaw:

The weapon's targeting system always selects the nearest enemy‚Äîtypically the most dangerous one players urgently need to eliminate. This creates a paradox where:

  • High-threat enemies become protected by the weapon meant to damage them
  • You cannot kill the tethered enemy until duration expires
  • The enemy continues moving, attacking, and dealing contact damage
  • No manual override exists to change targets

üìà Upgrade Paths (All Problematic)

Available Upgrades:

  • Damage - Only non-negative upgrade
  • Duration - AVOID (longer invulnerability)
  • Size - Slightly beneficial (wider beam)

Missing Upgrades:

  • Projectile Count (can't attach to multiple enemies)
  • Crit Chance/Damage
  • Attack Speed
  • Evolution mechanics

Best upgrade priority (if forced to use): Damage > Size > Never Duration

However, even with optimal upgrades, the weapon remains inferior to all alternatives due to its fundamental design flaw.
