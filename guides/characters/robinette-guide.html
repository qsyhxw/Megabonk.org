📊 Character Overview

💰 Unlock Method

Complete Forest Tier 2

🎯 Playstyle

Economy/Shop Optimization

⚔️ Default Weapon

Starting loadout ready

📈 Difficulty

Medium - Strategic planning

💎 Passive Ability: Golden Touch

Robinette's economy-focused passive provides significant advantages in gold generation and shop value:

  • Increased Gold Drops: Enemies drop more gold when killed
  • Shop Discount: Items in the shop cost less, enabling faster power scaling
  • Economy Snowball: More gold = more items = faster progression
  • Perfect for Long Runs: Wealth advantage compounds over time
💡 Core Identity: Robinette transforms the shop into your primary power source. While other characters rely on RNG drops, you buy your way to victory with strategic gold management and smart purchasing decisions.

✅ Strengths

  • Excellent shop value and item access
  • Consistent power scaling through purchases
  • Strong late-game economy dominance
  • Less RNG-dependent than other characters
  • Great for completing item collection
  • Synergizes with economic items

❌ Weaknesses

  • Slower early game without shop access
  • Requires strategic gold management
  • Less combat-focused passive
  • Weaker if shops are unlucky
  • Needs to reach shops to benefit
  • Gold items are mandatory picks

🎯 Recommended Builds

1. 💎 Maximum Wealth Scaling

Goal: Maximize gold generation to dominate shop purchases

Core Items (Priority Order):

  1. Piggy Bank - Core gold generation item
  2. Greed - Increases gold from all sources
  3. Lucky Coin - Chance for bonus gold drops
  4. Crown - Scaling gold per wave
  5. Metal Detector - Find more gold naturally

Supporting Stats:

  • Pickup Range - Collect gold efficiently
  • Luck - Better drops and shop rolls
  • Max HP - Survive to spend your wealth

Gameplan:

Focus entirely on gold generation in the early game. Every gold item you find should be purchased or picked up. Your goal is to reach a critical mass where you can buy every good item in every shop, creating an unstoppable power curve.

2. ⚡ Balanced Economy Fighter

Goal: Mix economy with combat power for consistent runs

Core Items (Priority Order):

  1. Piggy Bank / Greed - Pick one economy foundation
  2. Damage Weapon - Kevin, Dexecutioner, or similar
  3. Attack Speed Items - Scale your damage
  4. Lucky Coin - Passive gold generation
  5. Lifesteal / Regen - Sustainability

Supporting Stats:

  • Damage - Clear waves faster
  • Attack Speed - DPS scaling
  • Luck - Better shop options

Gameplan:

Don't neglect combat entirely. Pick 1-2 gold items early, then focus on damage scaling. Use your economic advantage to buy weapons and combat items that normally would be too expensive. This build is more forgiving and consistent.

3. 🛍️ Shop Reroll Master

Goal: Abuse shop rerolls to find perfect items

Core Items (Priority Order):

  1. Greed + Crown - Maximum gold income
  2. Luck Items - Better shop quality
  3. Any Gold Generation - Fund rerolls
  4. Microwave (if possible) - Duplicate purchases
  5. Meta Items - Kevin, Mirror, Timewarp

Supporting Stats:

  • Luck - Best shop items
  • Gold Generation - Reroll funding
  • XP Gain - More shops per run

Gameplan:

Save gold specifically for shop rerolls. Don't buy mediocre items - reroll until you see S-tier items. With Robinette's discounts, you can afford multiple rerolls per shop. This strategy finds the best possible items but requires patience and gold management.

⏱️ Strategy Timeline

0-5 Minutes: Foundation Phase

Goal: Survive early waves and prepare for first shop

  • Focus on basic survival - don't be greedy
  • Pick up ALL gold drops (Pickup Range helps)
  • Take any weapon that helps you clear safely
  • Look for your first gold generation item
  • Prioritize reaching shops over risky plays
💡 Early Game Tip: Robinette's passive doesn't help much before shops. Play conservatively and survive to capitalize on your economy advantage later.

5-15 Minutes: Economy Establishment

Goal: Build your gold engine and start purchasing power

  • Buy Piggy Bank or Greed in first shop if possible
  • Start stacking gold generation items
  • Use shop discounts to buy multiple items per visit
  • Balance economy items with survival needs
  • Reroll shops if no good options appear
💰 Gold Management: In shops, buy economy items FIRST, then spend remaining gold on combat items. Your gold generation compounds, so investments pay off quickly.

15-30 Minutes: Power Spike

Goal: Leverage your wealth advantage into combat dominance

  • You should have 2-3+ gold generation items by now
  • Start buying S-tier combat items (Kevin, Mirror, Timewarp)
  • Buy every good item in shops - you can afford it
  • Focus on completing synergistic combos
  • Maintain your economic lead with occasional gold picks
⚠️ Don't Over-Invest: Once you have 3-4 gold items, shift focus to combat. More gold doesn't help if you're dying. Balance is key.

30+ Minutes: Economic Domination

Goal: Buy everything and achieve perfect builds

  • Purchase every single good item in shops
  • Reroll aggressively for missing combo pieces
  • Gold is infinite - focus on optimization
  • Complete item collections and achievements
  • Experiment with different build paths
🏆 Late Game Power: With 4-5 gold items, you generate so much wealth that money becomes meaningless. Buy everything, reroll constantly, and build the perfect endgame setup.

💡 Pro Tips & Common Mistakes

✅ Advanced Techniques

  • Shop Timing: Plan routes to hit shops immediately after big gold waves for maximum purchasing power
  • Reroll Strategy: Don't reroll until you see the full shop first - might already have what you need
  • Item Valuation: Calculate if rerolling (costs gold) is worth it vs buying mediocre items
  • Pickup Range Synergy: More range = faster gold collection = faster shop purchasing
  • Luck Investment: Luck improves shop quality - worth investing in for Robinette
  • Gold Floor Strategy: Always maintain 50+ gold for emergency shop rerolls
  • Combo Completion: Use wealth to complete combos other characters can't afford
  • Microwave Abuse: If you find Microwave, duplicate expensive shop purchases for massive value
  • Crown Scaling: Crown gets better every wave - excellent late game investment
  • Shop Skip Decision: Sometimes worth skipping shops if items are bad - save gold for rerolls later

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Over-investing in Economy: 5+ gold items is overkill - you'll be rich but weak
  • Ignoring Survival: Gold means nothing if you're dead. Buy HP/Regen when needed
  • Not Using Discounts: Robinette's shop prices are lower - BUY MORE ITEMS per visit
  • Skipping Pickup Range: Slow gold collection wastes your economic advantage
  • Random Rerolling: Reroll with PURPOSE when looking for specific items, not randomly
  • Buying Every Shop Item: Early game, you can't afford everything. Be selective
  • Neglecting Weapons: Economy items don't kill enemies. Buy weapons too
  • Poor Gold Management: Spending all gold immediately leaves you unable to reroll
  • Ignoring Luck Stat: Luck directly improves your shop quality - very valuable
  • Fighting Without Items: Use your wealth - buy combat power, don't hoard gold
⚠️ Critical Mistake: The biggest error is building full economy with zero combat items. Robinette is about USING wealth to buy power, not just accumulating gold. If you have 500+ gold but weak damage, you're playing wrong.

🔓 How to Unlock Robinette

Unlock Requirement: Complete Forest Tier 2

Step 1: Reach Forest Biome

Select the Forest biome from the game menu. This is one of the starting biomes available to all players.

Step 2: Complete Tier 1

Successfully complete Forest Tier 1 difficulty. This unlocks Tier 2 access. Recommended for beginners to practice before attempting Tier 2.

Step 3: Attempt Forest Tier 2

Select Forest Tier 2 from the difficulty options. This tier has significantly stronger enemies and requires better builds.

  • Use a strong character like Fox or MegaChad
  • Focus on damage scaling and survival
  • Prioritize S-tier items from shops
  • Learn enemy patterns to avoid damage

Step 4: Complete the Run

Survive all waves and defeat the final boss. Upon completion, Robinette will be permanently unlocked for all future runs!

💡 Unlock Tips: Forest Tier 2 is moderately difficult. If struggling, practice with stronger characters first, learn optimal builds, and focus on damage + survival balance. Robinette is worth the effort!

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Robinette good for beginners?

Moderate difficulty. Robinette requires strategic thinking about economy management and when to invest vs when to spend. If you understand shop mechanics and item priorities, Robinette is very rewarding. If you prefer simpler "fight and win" characters, try Fox or MegaChad first.

How many gold items should I stack?

Optimal is 2-4 gold generation items. 2 is minimum for economy advantage, 3-4 is the sweet spot for wealth without sacrificing combat. 5+ is overkill and leaves you weak in fights. Balance is crucial.

Should I always reroll shops?

No. Reroll only when: (1) Shop has zero good items, (2) You're looking for specific combo pieces, or (3) You have excess gold late game. Early game, rerolling costs valuable gold needed for purchases.

What's the best first shop purchase?

Priority order: Piggy Bank > Greed > Any Gold Generation > Strong Weapon > Survival Items. Get your economy engine started first, then buy combat power with your growing wealth.

Does Robinette work on all biomes?

Yes! Economy builds work everywhere. Desert and Factory have more shops, making them slightly better for Robinette, but Forest is also good. Avoid biomes without shops if possible.

Can I complete challenges with Robinette?

Yes, though some challenges are harder. Robinette excels at "No Damage Taken" challenges (buy defensive items) and "Collect X Items" challenges (buy everything). Speed challenges are tougher since economy builds scale slowly.

Best late game strategy?

Once you have 4-5 gold items and generate 100+ gold per wave, buy EVERYTHING in shops. Reroll aggressively until you complete perfect combos. Use Microwave on expensive items if you find it. At this point, gold is infinite - focus on optimization.

Does Robinette's passive stack with gold items?

Yes! Robinette's increased gold generation and shop discounts work multiplicatively with items like Piggy Bank and Greed. This creates massive compound scaling - the core reason Robinette is S-tier for economy.

Why is Robinette A-tier instead of S-tier?

While Robinette dominates economy, the character lacks combat-focused passives. Characters like Dicehead (infinite crit scaling) or Bandit (attack speed scaling) have passives that directly improve fighting. Robinette's advantage is indirect - you must convert wealth into power through smart purchases.
