Stage clock and event order

Megabonk Timer Explained

Use the 10-minute stage window correctly, locate the Boss Portal before overtime, and understand why Final Swarm and the next-stage Portal are separate systems.

10:00 = OVERTIME PRESSURE

Quick Answer

A standard stage gives you a 10-minute preparation window. The Boss Portal is already placed at a random map location and can be found and activated early. When the normal window expires, Final Swarm / overtime pressure begins; defeating the Stage Boss controls whether a next-stage portal appears.

Standard Stage Timeline

0:00

Stage begins

Explore, collect XP, choose weapons and Tomes, and begin searching for the randomly placed Boss Portal.

3:00

First miniboss

Keep an open fighting area and avoid starting a risky Shrine charge just before this node.

4:00

First swarm event

Enemy pressure rises; judge whether clear speed is keeping pace.

7:00

Second swarm event

Finish optional routing and begin prioritizing boss-ready damage and defense.

8:00

Second miniboss

Treat the final two minutes as preparation rather than starting a long detour.

10:00

Overtime / Final Swarm

The normal stage window ends and survival pressure rises. The Boss Portal did not need to wait for this event.

These are the standard Forest/Desert reference nodes. Special maps, Crypt sections and challenge rules can use a different clock or objective.

Boss, Final Swarm and Portal Order

1. Find the Boss Portal

Search the random map location early and remember the return route.

2. Start the boss fight

Activate the portal when the build and health are ready.

3. Beat the deadline

At 10:00, overtime or Final Swarm pressure begins whether or not the boss route is complete.

4. Choose the next step

After the kill, use a stage portal only when another stage exists.

Common mistake: “Boss Portal” and “next-stage Portal” are not interchangeable. The first starts the boss encounter; the second advances the run after the boss and only exists when the selected tier has another stage. See the Boss Portal troubleshooting guide.

Timer Differences by Mode

Standard Forest / Desert stages

Use the timeline above. Find and activate the Boss Portal before or during the 10-minute window; overtime pressure begins when that window expires.

Graveyard Crypt

The Crypt uses a countdown. When it reaches zero, a short grace window begins before Spooky Steve appears. Follow the Graveyard route, not the standard 10-minute timeline.

Tier 1 endpoint

There is no next-stage progression portal because the selected run contains only one stage. The survival phase remains relevant.

Tier 2 and Tier 3

A next-stage portal appears after a qualifying stage boss when another stage remains. Entering it advances the route.

Timer FAQ

What does the timer mean?

On standard stages it tracks the preparation window and scheduled pressure events before overtime begins.

What happens at 10 minutes?

The normal stage window ends and Final Swarm or overtime pressure begins. The Boss Portal can be found and activated earlier.

When does the next-stage Portal open?

After the Stage Boss is defeated, and only when the selected tier has another stage.

Does the timer reset on the next stage?

Each standard stage runs its own event timeline. Treat a new stage as a fresh preparation cycle.

Why is the Crypt timer counting down?

It is a different map mechanic. Reach the exit before zero when possible; zero begins a short grace period before Spooky Steve appears.
