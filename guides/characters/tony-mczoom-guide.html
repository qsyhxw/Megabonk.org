He excels at high-speed kiting and hit-and-run tactics, but his fragility and weapon difficulty make him challenging for newer players. Success with Tony requires excellent positioning, constant movement, and mastery of the Wireless Dagger's homing projectiles.

⚡ Passive Ability: Zap

Official Description: "Charge an electric attack by moving around. Gain 2% Pickup Range per level."

How Zap Works

Tony McZoom's passive ability is centered around his hoverboard movement. As Tony moves around the battlefield, he builds up an electrical charge that periodically discharges, dealing damage to nearby enemies in an area around him.

Key Mechanics

  • Movement-Based Charging: Moving on the hoverboard charges up the electric attack
  • Electric Discharge: When charged, releases an AOE electric blast around Tony
  • Pickup Range Scaling: Gain +2% Pickup Range per character level
  • Turns Movement into Damage: The more you move, the more Zap procs occur
💡 Synergy Tip: Tony's Zap ability naturally synergizes with his high-speed playstyle. Since you'll be constantly moving to avoid damage, you'll automatically be charging and releasing Zap attacks. This makes Tony excellent at clearing groups of weaker enemies while kiting.

Leveling Benefits

As Tony levels up during a run, the +2% Pickup Range per level becomes increasingly valuable. This allows you to collect items, gold, and experience while maintaining your speed and distance from enemies - you don't need to slow down or get close to pick things up.

📋 Strategy & Gameplay Tips

Early Game (0-10 Minutes)

  • Your speed advantage is your best survival tool - use it
  • Practice kiting enemies in circles and figure-8 patterns
  • Let Wireless Dagger's homing do the work - don't aim too precisely
  • Collect gold aggressively with your speed advantage
  • Focus on dodging rather than tanking any damage

Mid Game (10-20 Minutes)

  • Prioritize Projectile Speed and Quantity in shops
  • Add Turbo Socks or Turbo Skates if available
  • Start stacking Critical Chance for damage scaling
  • Your Zap passive should be clearing weak enemies passively
  • Maintain constant movement to charge Zap frequently

Late Game (20+ Minutes)

  • You should be nearly untouchable with speed stacking
  • Focus on damage multipliers - Crit Damage, Damage %
  • Consider adding Lightning Staff or Aura as secondary weapons
  • Use Aegis for additional survivability if needed
  • Your Pickup Range should be massive - collect everything safely
🎯 Advanced Technique: "Speed Kiting Circles" - Run in large circles around enemy groups. Your homing daggers will track enemies while you maintain maximum distance. The Zap passive will damage enemies as you pass through the outskirts of their formation. This technique makes Tony nearly invincible when executed properly.

💡 Pro Tips & Common Mistakes

✅ Do This

  • Keep Moving: Standing still wastes your speed advantage and stops Zap charging
  • Master Wireless Dagger: Let the homing do its job, focus on positioning
  • Stack Projectile Stats: Your weapon scales incredibly well with projectile items
  • Use Diagonal Movement: Move diagonally to maximize distance from enemies
  • Collect Items While Moving: Your Pickup Range bonus means you never need to stop
  • Practice Circle Kiting: Run circles around enemies - they'll chase but never catch you
  • Add Movement Speed Items: Turbo Socks and Turbo Skates are excellent on Tony
  • Watch Your Positioning: Use your speed to control engagement distances

❌ Don't Do This

  • Don't Stand Still: You're fragile - movement is your defense
  • Don't Ignore Projectile Stats: Your weapon needs these to shine
  • Don't Face-Tank: You have low HP - avoid all damage when possible
  • Don't Over-Speed: Too much movement speed becomes hard to control
  • Don't Fight in Corners: You need space to kite effectively
  • Don't Neglect HP Items: Some survivability helps compensate for fragility
  • Don't Use Tony as First Character: He's not beginner-friendly
  • Don't Forget About Zap: Your passive does significant damage - charge it constantly
⚠️ Critical Mistake: The biggest error with Tony McZoom is treating him like a tankier character. Tony has LOW DEFENSE and requires constant movement to survive. Players who try to stand and fight will die quickly. Always be moving, always be kiting, and trust your speed to keep you safe.

🔓 How to Unlock Tony McZoom

Unlock Requirements:
  1. Complete ANY 2 challenges from the Challenge menu
  2. Unlock the Wireless Dagger weapon (reach Level 15 with Lightning Staff in a run)

Step-by-Step Unlock Guide

Part 1: Complete 2 Challenges

Open the Challenge menu and complete any 2 challenges. Recommended easy challenges:

  • Kill 100 Enemies - Happens naturally in any run
  • Reach Wave 5 - Easy progression goal
  • Collect 25 Items - Happens automatically
  • Deal 5,000 Total Damage - Very easy to achieve
💡 Quick Tip: Many challenges can be completed simultaneously in a single run. "Kill 100 enemies" and "Reach Wave 5" can both be done in one 10-minute game. Use strong characters like Fox or MegaChad to make completing challenges easier.

Part 2: Unlock Wireless Dagger

Before you can use Tony McZoom, you must first unlock his starting weapon:

  1. Start a run with any character
  2. Acquire the Lightning Staff weapon (common early weapon)
  3. Level Lightning Staff to Level 15 during the run
  4. Wireless Dagger will permanently unlock
⚡ Lightning Staff Tip: Lightning Staff is a common weapon that shoots electric bolts. It's easy to level by simply using it throughout a run. Focus on surviving to late game (20+ minutes) and you'll naturally reach level 15 with it.

After Unlocking

Once you've completed 2 challenges AND unlocked Wireless Dagger, Tony McZoom will be permanently available in your character roster. He'll start every run with Wireless Dagger equipped.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Tony McZoom good for beginners?

No. Despite being relatively easy to unlock, Tony is not beginner-friendly. He's fragile, requires good positioning skills, and the Wireless Dagger weapon is difficult to master. Start with tankier characters like MegaChad or simpler weapons before trying Tony.

Why is Tony only B-tier?

Tony McZoom ranks B-tier because while he has exceptional mobility, he lacks the raw damage output and survivability of S-tier and A-tier characters. His Wireless Dagger is difficult to master, and his fragility means mistakes are heavily punished. He's viable and fun but not top-tier competitive.

What makes Tony's speed special?

Tony rides a hoverboard and is officially the fastest character in MegaBonk. This speed advantage allows for superior kiting, positioning, and safety. His Zap passive also charges based on movement, so his speed directly translates to more damage output.

Can I use Tony without Wireless Dagger?

You cannot play Tony without first unlocking Wireless Dagger - it's his starting weapon and required for unlock. However, during a run you can pick up other weapons to replace or supplement it. That said, Wireless Dagger synergizes well with his mobile playstyle due to its homing projectiles.

How does the Zap passive work exactly?

Zap charges as you move around on the hoverboard. When fully charged, it releases an electric AOE attack around Tony that damages nearby enemies. The more you move, the more frequently Zap procs. It's an automatic ability that rewards Tony's constant movement playstyle.

What's the best build for Tony?

The Projectile Speed Build is generally strongest, focusing on Projectile Speed, Quantity, and Size to make Wireless Dagger extremely effective. Add Critical Chance and Movement Speed items, and you'll have a powerful, safe build that leverages all of Tony's strengths.

How do I control Tony at high speeds?

Practice is key. Start with moderate movement speed and gradually add more as you get comfortable. Use large, sweeping circular movements rather than tight turns. Diagonal movement gives you more control than pure vertical/horizontal. Don't stack too much movement speed (150%+) until you're experienced with Tony.

Should I focus on damage or speed?

Both. Speed is your survivability tool and charges Zap, but you also need damage to kill enemies efficiently. Prioritize Projectile Stats (Speed/Quantity) and Movement Speed early, then add damage multipliers (Crit Chance, Damage %) in mid-late game.

Best characters to unlock before Tony?

Fox, MegaChad, and Robinette are all easier to play and will help you learn game mechanics. These characters are more forgiving and will prepare you for Tony's high-skill, fragile playstyle.

What should I do if I keep dying as Tony?

You're probably not moving enough or getting cornered. Tony requires CONSTANT movement - never stand still. Practice kiting in large circles, avoid corners and edges, and add some Max HP items for a survivability buffer. Remember: movement is your defense, not armor.
