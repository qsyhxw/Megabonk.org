Is Megabonk Multiplayer or Co-op? Current Status (2026)

Last verified: July 23, 2026 · Current game version: v1.0.69

No. Megabonk v1.0.69 does not support native multiplayer or co-op. Steam currently lists the game as Single-player, with no official online co-op, local or couch co-op, split-screen, PvP, matchmaking, or Remote Play Together. Steam Link and Remote Play Anywhere can stream the same solo session to another device, while unofficial mods such as BonkWithFriends can add experimental P2P co-op at your own risk.

Remote Play Does Not Add Co-op

Remote Play Anywhere and the Steam Link app let you run Megabonk on a host PC and stream its video, audio, and controls to your phone, tablet, TV, or another computer. That can be useful for playing away from the host PC, but it is still the same one-player run.

Remote Play Together is different: it shares a game's existing local multiplayer session with remote friends. Because Megabonk has no native local co-op or second playable character, Remote Play Together cannot turn the vanilla game into simultaneous co-op. At most, ordinary screen sharing or control handoff can let friends watch or take turns.

See Valve's official Steam Remote Play explanation for the distinction between device streaming and Remote Play Together.

That’s why you’ll see plenty of forum threads, Reddit posts and guide articles all circling around the same core question: “This game is awesome—can I play it with my friends?”

How to “Play Megabonk with Friends” Anyway

Even though Megabonk doesn’t have built-in co-op, there are still plenty of ways to enjoy it together with friends.

1. Race Each Other Using the Same Rules

You can treat Megabonk like a shared challenge game:

  • Pick the same character and map.
  • Agree on some simple rules: “No rerolls”, “No anvil”, “No defensive tomes”, etc.
  • Start your run at the same time in voice chat and see who:
    • Survives the longest
    • Reaches a certain wave fastest
    • Gets the highest kill count or gold

This “parallel run” style is already popular in the community and scratches a similar itch to co-op: you’re reacting to the same chaos, comparing builds, and trash-talking in real time, even though you’re technically in separate runs.

2. Compete on Leaderboards and Achievements

Megabonk is built around competitive leaderboards, rich stats, and a huge list of achievements, which naturally lend themselves to friendly rivalry.

  • Pick a category (e.g., “fastest clear on a certain map” or “highest score with a specific character”).
  • Add your friends or community members.
  • Refresh the leaderboard and try to snipe each other’s placements week after week.

For easier comparison outside the game client, you can use external leaderboard views or curated “top runs” pages if you have them—for example: Megabonk.org leaderboard hub.

3. Share and Copy Builds

Another low-friction way to “play together” is to share build ideas:

  • Post screenshots of your best runs, items, tomes, and upgrades.
  • Challenge friends to recreate your build and beat your score.
  • Use community tier lists and build guides to create “rulesets” that everyone must follow.

Can a Mod Add Megabonk Multiplayer?

Yes, but it is unofficial. As checked on July 23, 2026, BonkWithFriends 0.4.1 on Thunderstore adds drop-in/drop-out co-op using Steamworks P2P. Its listed features include Steam friend invites, world and combat synchronization, revives, spectator mode, and in-game chat.

This does not change the official status of Megabonk. The mod requires third-party tooling, is not supported by the developer, and its own page says it remains in active development.

Before Installing a Multiplayer Mod

  • Expect patch risk: a Megabonk update can require a compatible mod update.
  • Expect possible desync or bugs: multiplayer state is added by the mod, not the vanilla game.
  • Back up your save: keep a clean profile or backup before changing the game installation.
  • Keep modded runs off official leaderboards: use vanilla for comparable score submissions.
  • Download only from the project's listed page: avoid reuploads and unknown installers.

For the most stable experience, use vanilla single-player. Use community multiplayer only when every player accepts the setup work and the possibility of version-specific problems.
