🔍 Weapon Overview

The Shotgun is a directional spread weapon that fires multiple pellets in a cone pattern, requiring manual aim but delivering devastating close-range burst damage. Unlike auto-targeting weapons like Katana or Aura, players must position and aim carefully with their cursor. When all pellets connect at close range, the Shotgun can instantly wipe out swarms, making it one of the most powerful burst damage weapons in Megabonk—if you can handle the aim requirement and unlock RNG.

📊 Tier Rating

B-tier - Strong with proper aim and build investment, but RNG unlock and manual aim requirement prevent universal adoption

🔓 Unlock Method

5% drop chance from breaking Tumbleweeds on Desert Stage 2

💰 Purchase Cost

2 Silver from Main Menu → Unlocks tab after meeting unlock condition

🎯 Targeting Type

Manual Aim - Direction controlled by mouse cursor

⚙️ Core Mechanics

💥 Spread Pattern & Pellet System

The Shotgun fires multiple pellets in a cone-shaped spread centered on your cursor direction. Each pellet is an independent projectile that can damage enemies. Unlike weapons that fire single projectiles, the Shotgun's strength comes from multiple pellets hitting the same target simultaneously, creating extreme burst damage potential at close range.

Key mechanic: The closer you are to enemies, the more pellets will connect with a single target. At point-blank range, 100% of pellets can hit a single enemy for maximum damage. At mid-range, pellets spread and only some hit. At long range, the spread becomes too wide for reliable damage.

🎯 Manual Aim Requirement

Unlike smart-targeting weapons (Katana, Wireless Dagger, Aura), you must manually aim the Shotgun with your mouse cursor. This adds a skill requirement but provides precise control over positioning. Players comfortable with manual aim will find the Shotgun highly rewarding, while those who prefer fire-and-forget weapons should avoid it.

📈 Upgrade Paths

  • 🎯 Projectile Count - HIGHEST PRIORITY. Increases the number of pellets fired per shot, dramatically improving damage potential and spread coverage. Stack this early and often.
  • ⏱️ Cooldown - HIGH PRIORITY. Reduces time between shots, allowing rapid-fire shotgun blasts. Essential for maintaining pressure in swarms.
  • 💪 Damage - Standard damage scaling that affects all pellets. Good universal upgrade.
  • 🔄 Projectile Speed - Increases pellet travel velocity. Generally low priority since Shotgun excels at close range where speed matters less.
  • 📏 Size - May increase pellet hitbox size. Useful for consistency but lower priority than Count/Cooldown.

⚠️ Critical Upgrade Priority

Always prioritize Projectile Count and Cooldown upgrades. These two stats multiplicatively increase the Shotgun's effectiveness more than any other stats. Without high projectile count, the weapon's spread becomes too inconsistent. Without cooldown reduction, you can't maintain pressure during swarm phases.

🔓 How to Unlock the Shotgun

The Shotgun has one of the most RNG-dependent unlock methods in Megabonk. Unlike quest-based or achievement-based unlocks, you're at the mercy of random chance—specifically a 5% drop rate from Tumbleweeds on Desert Stage 2.

📝 Step-by-Step Unlock Guide

  1. Reach Desert Stage 2

    Progress through the Desert map to Stage 2. This typically requires defeating the Desert Stage 1 boss first.

  2. Find and Break Tumbleweeds

    Tumbleweeds are rolling environmental objects that appear on the Desert map. They look like rolling bushes and can be destroyed by attacking them. You must chase and break Tumbleweeds specifically on Stage 2—Stage 1 Tumbleweeds drop the Katana instead.

  3. Hope for 5% Drop Chance

    Each Tumbleweed you break on Desert Stage 2 has a 5% chance to unlock the Shotgun. This means on average, you'll need to break 20 Tumbleweeds to get the unlock. Some players get it on their first try; others spend hours farming.

  4. No Weapon Slot Required

    Unlike some unlocks, you do NOT need an empty weapon slot for the Shotgun to unlock. The unlock happens in the background and you'll see a notification. You can even die after getting the unlock—it's permanent.

  5. Purchase from Main Menu

    After unlocking, return to the Main Menu and go to Unlocks tab → Weapons. Purchase the Shotgun for 2 Silver. It will then be available in future runs.

💡 Unlock Optimization Tips

  • Use a fast character (Calcium, Tony McZoom) to chase Tumbleweeds more easily
  • Take movement speed upgrades from shrines and items (Turbo Socks)
  • Don't rush the stage—take your time to explore every corner for Tumbleweeds
  • Multiple runs expected—Most players report 3-10 runs before the drop
  • Can be done on any difficulty—Lower difficulty = easier survival while farming

⚠️ Common Frustrations

"I've broken 50 Tumbleweeds and still nothing!" - This is RNG. At 5%, you could theoretically never get the drop (though extremely unlikely after 50). Most players get it within 20 breaks. Make sure you're on Desert Stage 2, not Stage 1—Stage 1 drops Katana instead.

👥 Character Synergies

🔥 Best Characters for Shotgun

🗡️ Bandit ★★★★★ S-Tier Synergy

Why Bandit: Bandit's passive grants +1% Attack Speed per level, which directly translates to faster Shotgun shots. By level 50, Bandit fires Shotguns 50% faster than other characters. Combined with Cooldown Tome, Bandit can create a rapid-fire shotgun build that deletes bosses in seconds.

Recommended Build: Shotgun + Revolver + Bow + Bananarang | Cooldown Tome + Quantity Tome + Damage Tome + XP Tome

🎯 CL4NK ★★★★ A-Tier Synergy

Why CL4NK: CL4NK gains +1% Crit Chance per level, and each Shotgun pellet can crit independently. High projectile count means dozens of pellets, each with crit chance, creating massive burst damage potential. CL4NK's starter weapon Revolver also synergizes with crit builds.

Recommended Build: Shotgun + Revolver + Dice + Aegis | Precision Tome + Quantity Tome + Damage Tome + XP Tome

Key Items: Giant Fork (Megacrits), Forbidden Juice (crit chance), Gym Sauce (damage), Demonic Blade (lifesteal on crit)

🛡️ Sir Oofie ★★★ B-Tier Synergy

Why Sir Oofie: Sir Oofie's +1% Armor per level passive allows aggressive close-range positioning required for the Shotgun. Beginners benefit from the tankiness, letting them get close enough for all pellets to connect without dying instantly.

Recommended Build: Shotgun + Aegis + Aura + Lightning Staff | Quantity Tome + Cooldown Tome + Armor Tome + HP Tome

Key Items: Chonkplate (overheal), Beefy Ring (damage from HP), Medkit (regen), Oats (HP)

⚠️ Characters to Avoid

⚔️ Combat Strategy & Positioning

🎯 Optimal Range Management

  • Point-Blank Range (0-3 meters): Maximum damage potential. All pellets hit single target. High risk but highest reward. Use against bosses and elite enemies.
  • Close Range (3-6 meters): Good damage with partial pellet spread. Balances risk and reward. Ideal for swarms.
  • Mid Range (6-10 meters): Moderate damage. Pellets spread significantly. Backup range when overwhelmed.
  • Long Range (10+ meters): Ineffective. Pellets too spread out. Switch to backup weapons.

    • 🏃 Movement Patterns

    ✅ The Shotgun Circle Strafe

    The optimal movement pattern for Shotgun users involves circular strafing around enemy groups. Move in a tight circle at close range while firing into the center of the swarm. This keeps enemies grouped, ensures all pellets connect, and makes dodging predictable.

    🧩 Terrain Usage

    • Funnel Enemies: Use walls and obstacles to create choke points where enemies must approach from one direction. Blast the funnel with Shotgun.
    • Corner Control: Position yourself in corners to limit enemy approach angles. Forces enemies into tight groups perfect for Shotgun spread.
    • Kiting Loops: Create circular kiting paths that bring you past enemy groups repeatedly, allowing drive-by Shotgun blasts.

    👹 Boss Fight Strategy

    💡 Boss-Specific Tactics

    • Phase 1 (High HP): Aggressive circle strafe at close range. Unload Shotgun point-blank. Dodge boss special attacks but maintain proximity.
    • Phase 2 (Low HP): Boss becomes more aggressive. Use backup weapons (Bow, Aura) at mid-range. Shotgun for burst windows when safe.
    • Boss Minions: Shotgun excels at clearing boss-spawned adds. One shot can clear multiple minions.

    🌊 Swarm Management

    Shotgun is a swarm-clearing weapon. Against dense crowds, position yourself in the middle (with proper defenses) and fire in different directions. Each Shotgun blast can damage 5-15+ enemies simultaneously depending on projectile count and spread.

    ⚖️ Strengths & Weaknesses

    ✅ Strengths

    • Highest burst damage potential at close range when all pellets connect
    • Exceptional swarm clearing with proper Projectile Count scaling
    • Manual aim provides precise control over damage direction
    • Scales exponentially with Projectile Count and Cooldown upgrades
    • Strong synergy with attack speed characters (Bandit) and crit builds (CL4NK)
    • Can one-shot elite enemies and severely damage bosses with optimized builds
    • Works well with on-hit effect items (Moldy Cheese, Ice Crystal) due to multiple pellets
    • Satisfying visual and gameplay feel when fully upgraded

    ❌ Weaknesses

    • RNG-dependent unlock (5% Tumbleweed drop) can take hours of farming
    • Requires manual aim skill—not beginner-friendly like auto-targeting weapons
    • Forces close-range aggressive positioning, increasing risk of death
    • Ineffective at long range due to pellet spread
    • Heavily build-dependent—weak without Projectile Count and Cooldown investment
    • Requires backup weapons for range coverage (Bow, Lightning Staff)
    • Not competitive for world record runs compared to meta weapons (Dexecutioner, Katana)
    • Struggles in early game before upgrade scaling kicks in
    • Poor single-target damage at distance—designed for close-range swarms

    💡 Common Questions

    ❓ FAQ

    • Q: Do I need to kill enemies with Tumbleweeds to unlock?
      A: No. You just need to break Tumbleweeds on Desert Stage 2. The 5% chance triggers on breaking them, not killing with them.
    • Q: Can I unlock Shotgun on Desert Stage 1?
      A: No. Desert Stage 1 Tumbleweeds drop Katana. Shotgun only drops from Desert Stage 2 Tumbleweeds.
    • Q: Is Shotgun better than Revolver?
      A: Different use cases. Revolver has better range and crit scaling but lower burst. Shotgun has highest burst at close range but weak at distance.
    • Q: Does Projectile Speed matter for Shotgun?
      A: Not really. Shotgun excels at close range where pellet travel time is minimal. Focus on Projectile Count and Cooldown instead.

    ⚔️ Weapon Comparisons

    🔫 Shotgun vs. Revolver

    Both are projectile-based manual aim weapons but serve different roles:

    • Range: Revolver wins. Works well at all ranges. Shotgun is close-range only.
    • Burst Damage: Shotgun wins at close range. Revolver more consistent.
    • Crit Scaling: Revolver has native crit stats. Shotgun must rely on character/items.
    • Ease of Use: Tie—both require manual aim.
    • Meta Viability: Revolver S/A-tier, Shotgun B-tier.

    Verdict: Use both together in builds for close/mid-range coverage.

    🗡️ Shotgun vs. Dexecutioner

    Two high-damage burst weapons with different mechanics:

    • Damage Type: Dexecutioner has execute mechanic (instant kill <20% HP). Shotgun is pure burst.
    • Range: Dexecutioner works at all ranges. Shotgun close-range only.
    • Consistency: Dexecutioner more reliable. Shotgun requires aim.
    • Swarm Clearing: Shotgun better for dense swarms. Dexecutioner better for elites.

    Verdict: Dexecutioner is objectively better for competitive play but Shotgun more satisfying when it works.

    🏹 Shotgun vs. Bow

    • Range: Bow pierces through multiple enemies at any range. Shotgun close-range only.
    • Damage Pattern: Bow is single line pierce. Shotgun is cone spread.
    • Ease of Use: Bow easier—less precision needed.
    • Build Dependency: Bow works with minimal upgrades. Shotgun needs heavy investment.

    Verdict: Bow is more versatile and beginner-friendly. Shotgun has higher ceiling with proper build.

    • You enjoy manual aim mechanics and skill-based gameplay
    • You're playing Bandit or CL4NK for optimal synergy
    • You want the highest burst damage in the game at close range
    • You're willing to invest in Projectile Count and Cooldown upgrades
    • You have AoE backup weapons (Aura, Lightning Staff) for coverage
    • You're farming Tier 1-2 content where close-range aggression is safe

    • You prefer fire-and-forget auto-targeting weapons
    • You're attempting world record leaderboard runs (use meta weapons instead)
    • You're still learning the game and die frequently (use safer ranged options)
    • You don't want to spend hours farming RNG Tumbleweed drops
    • You're on Tier 3 endless content where close-range is too risky
    • You prefer consistent DPS over burst damage spikes

    📊 Overall Rating: B-Tier

    The Shotgun is a powerful, satisfying weapon that rewards skill and proper investment but cannot compete with top-tier meta options for competitive play. It delivers the highest burst damage in the game when all pellets connect at point-blank range, and with Bandit's attack speed scaling or CL4NK's crit synergy, it can create devastating builds that trivialize mid-tier content.

    However, the weapon's close-range requirement, manual aim demand, and RNG unlock frustration prevent it from achieving universal popularity. Players comfortable with aggressive positioning and precise aim will find the Shotgun incredibly rewarding, while those seeking safer, more consistent weapons should look elsewhere.

    Unlock it if you enjoy high-risk, high-reward gameplay. The satisfaction of landing a perfect point-blank Shotgun blast that instantly clears a screen of enemies is unmatched in Megabonk. Just don't expect it to carry you to the top of the leaderboards—that's what Dexecutioner and Katana are for.
