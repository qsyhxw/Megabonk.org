⚙️ Mechanics (verified)

No speculative numbers; only widely confirmed behavior

🔥 Fire Trail

While moving, you leave a burning trail that damages enemies over time. When you stop, no new trail is created; existing trails continue to burn briefly.

Tip: Use loops and revisits to keep enemies inside existing fire zones longer.

🏃 Movement-Driven Damage

Effectiveness scales with your pathing and coverage. Circles, spirals, and figure-8s increase overlap and uptime on elite targets.

📈 Upgrades

Upgrades commonly affect damage, duration, size (area), and sometimes the frequency/instances of effects. Avoid hard-coding per-level percentages unless patch notes confirm them.

⏱️ Tome Interactions

Agility, Cooldown, Quantity, Size, and Damage typically raise overall coverage density and sustained DPS by increasing speed, frequency, area, or base power.

🏗️ Recommended Builds

Examples based on common community approaches (not the only way)

🌪️ Endless Kite
🎯 Core Plan

Keep moving in wide circles or slow spirals to paint the map with fire. Re-enter old trails to keep elites burning.

📚 Tomes (priority)
  • Agility (movement speed)
  • Cooldown (attack speed / more frequent effects)
  • Quantity or Size
  • Damage
🎒 Items (examples)
  • Turbo Socks (boosts movement speed)
  • Turbo Skates (movement benefits attack cadence)
  • Slippery Ring (raises evasion)
  • Phantom Shroud (post-dodge burst and mobility)
  • Overpowered Lamp (improves proc/trigger reliability)
🧩 Side Weapons
  • Aura (locks enemies near you) or Lightning Staff (fast trash clear)
🔥 Inferno Figure-8
🎯 Core Plan

Carve compact figure-8 or “petal” loops to stack dense burn pockets for bossing and elite waves.

📚 Tomes
  • Quantity / Size (overlap & coverage)
  • Cooldown
  • Damage
🎒 Items (examples)
  • Overpowered Lamp (steadier elemental/trigger builds)
  • Turbo Socks / Slippery Ring (mobility & safety)
🧩 Side Weapons
  • Aura (keeps enemies inside the hot zone) or Lightning Staff (finisher)

⚖️ Comparisons (playstyle focus)

Not a strict “better/worse”; emphasizes role and complement

Flamewalker vs Aura

Flamewalker: Map-wide pathing and denial while moving.

Aura: Constant close-range damage even when stationary; good safety net.

Flamewalker vs Lightning Staff

Flamewalker: Lingering damage zones, great for kiting and attrition.

Lightning: Snappier trash clear and early tempo; complements Flamewalker well.
