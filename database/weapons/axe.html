üéØ Weapon Overview

The Axe is one of Megabonk's most notorious weapons - widely criticized as the worst starting weapon, but praised as potentially the most broken late-game weapon. This spinning projectile weapon throws axes that pierce through enemies, and when properly built with high projectile count and attack speed, it becomes an absolute screen-clearing monster.

Why the reputation split? The Axe starts extremely weak without any buffs, making early-game survival challenging. However, once you stack projectile count and attack speed upgrades, it transforms into one of the most powerful weapons in the game, capable of "murdering any map very easily" according to experienced players.

üéØ The "Axe Scaling Formula"

Understanding the math behind why Axe becomes so broken:

Base DPS Formula:

Total DPS = (Base Damage √ó Projectile Count √ó Attack Speed √ó Hit Rate √ó Duration Multiplier)

Why Axe Scales Exponentially:

  • Projectile Count: Each axe can hit multiple enemies multiple times
  • Attack Speed: More throws per second = more axes on screen simultaneously
  • Duration: Axes stay active longer = more total hits
  • Size: Bigger axes = higher hit rate per throw

Example: With 10 projectiles, 5 attacks per second, and 3-second duration, you have 150 axes spinning on screen at once. Each one can hit multiple enemies. That's why late-game Axe is "ridiculously OP."

üéÆ Playstyle Tips

Early Game (Levels 1-10):

  • Play defensively - prioritize survival over kills
  • Take ANY projectile count upgrade immediately
  • Use shrines for Attack Speed when available
  • Consider picking up a secondary weapon (Bow, Lightning Staff)
  • Focus on reaching level 10 safely

Mid Game (Levels 10-20):

  • Continue stacking Projectile Count (aim for 8-10 axes)
  • Balance Attack Speed with Size upgrades
  • Start taking Damage % from shrines
  • Look for Anvil item to boost all upgrades
  • Farm XP aggressively to reach late game faster

Late Game (Level 20+):

  • Max out Projectile Count (15+ axes)
  • Push Attack Speed to cap if possible
  • You're now unstoppable - just walk through mobs
  • Boss fights: Circle the boss while axes constantly hit
  • Final swarm: Stand still and let your axe wall protect you

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is the Axe really that bad early game?

A: Yes, it's widely considered the worst starting weapon. Without projectile count and attack speed upgrades, it feels weak and unreliable. But this is intentional design - it's meant to be a "high investment, high reward" weapon.

Q: How many projectiles do I need for Axe to feel good?

A: Around 6-8 axes is when it starts feeling powerful. At 10+ axes with good attack speed, it becomes one of the best weapons in the game.

Q: Should I use Axe on my first playthrough?

A: No. Start with easier weapons like Sword, Firestaff, or Bow. Learn the game mechanics first, then try Axe once you understand upgrade priorities.

Q: What's the best character for Axe?

A: Ogre is the best (it's his default weapon + damage scaling). Sir Oofie is good for beginners due to survivability.

Q: Can Axe work without projectile stacking?

A: Technically yes, but you're missing the weapon's entire potential. Projectile Count is THE defining stat for Axe. Without it, use a different weapon.

Q: Is the Axe + Bow combo really that good?

A: Yes. Community consensus is this combination is "ridiculously OP" because both weapons benefit from the same stat investments (Projectile Count and Attack Speed). It's one of the strongest weapon combos in the game.
