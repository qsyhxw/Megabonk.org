Is Megabonk Multiplayer? Co-op, Mods & Online Play Explained

Last updated: March 10, 2026 · Megabonk FAQ & guides

As of November 2025, Megabonk is a single-player game only. There is no built-in online multiplayer, couch co-op, or PvP mode. The solo developer has said in community discussions that multiplayer is not planned for the game right now, but it may be explored in the future if it fits the game’s design.

Short Answer: Is Megabonk Multiplayer Right Now?

If you’re wondering whether you can jump into Megabonk with your friends in the same run, the answer is simple:

Feature Available in Megabonk?
Game mode Single-player only
Online co-op No
Local / couch co-op No
PvP / duels No

The Steam store page and multiple third-party guides list Megabonk as a single-player survival roguelike, focused on solo runs, build crafting, and leaderboard chasing rather than on co-op systems or matchmaking.

For now, if you buy Megabonk, you’re buying a high-tempo, solo action roguelike experience.

Why Do So Many Players Ask for Multiplayer?

It’s very natural that “Is Megabonk multiplayer?” is one of the most common questions around the game. There are a few reasons:

  • Genre expectations. Megabonk lives in the same general space as other horde-survivor and action roguelike games. Many titles in this space either already have co-op or receive constant “co-op when?” requests, so players assume it might be planned here too.
  • Huge player base. Since launch, Megabonk has pulled in a massive audience on Steam, with tens of thousands of concurrent players and very high review scores. In communities this large, a big share of players naturally want to play together, not just side-by-side in single player.
  • Perfect “chaos with friends” fantasy. The over-the-top builds, silly enemies and screen-filling projectiles make Megabonk look like the kind of game that would be hilarious with friends shouting over each other in voice chat.

That’s why you’ll see plenty of forum threads, Reddit posts and guide articles all circling around the same core question: “This game is awesome—can I play it with my friends?”

Dev Comments & Future Plans for Multiplayer

So what has the developer actually said?

  • In community discussions summarized by gaming sites, the developer has explained that multiplayer “isn’t for the game right now”, and that the current focus is on performance, balance, and core content.
  • At the same time, those reports note that the dev is interested in looking into multiplayer in the future, alongside mod support and other long-term features. There is no official roadmap or release window for any kind of co-op mode.

In other words:

Multiplayer is not part of the current, supported version of Megabonk. It’s something the dev has talked about as a possible future direction, but with no promises or dates attached.

Given that Megabonk is developed by a single creator and is still receiving frequent patches to its core systems, it’s realistic to assume that any official multiplayer—if it happens at all—would arrive as a major long-term update, not a quick feature toggle.

How to “Play Megabonk with Friends” Anyway

Even though Megabonk doesn’t have built-in co-op, there are still plenty of ways to enjoy it together with friends.

1. Race Each Other Using the Same Rules

You can treat Megabonk like a shared challenge game:

  • Pick the same character and map.
  • Agree on some simple rules: “No rerolls”, “No anvil”, “No defensive tomes”, etc.
  • Start your run at the same time in voice chat and see who:
    • Survives the longest
    • Reaches a certain wave fastest
    • Gets the highest kill count or gold

This “parallel run” style is already popular in the community and scratches a similar itch to co-op: you’re reacting to the same chaos, comparing builds, and trash-talking in real time, even though you’re technically in separate runs.

2. Compete on Leaderboards and Achievements

Megabonk is built around competitive leaderboards, rich stats, and a huge list of achievements, which naturally lend themselves to friendly rivalry.

  • Pick a category (e.g., “fastest clear on a certain map” or “highest score with a specific character”).
  • Add your friends or community members.
  • Refresh the leaderboard and try to snipe each other’s placements week after week.

For easier comparison outside the game client, you can use external leaderboard views or curated “top runs” pages if you have them—for example: Megabonk.org leaderboard hub.

3. Share and Copy Builds

Another low-friction way to “play together” is to share build ideas:

  • Post screenshots of your best runs, items, tomes, and upgrades.
  • Challenge friends to recreate your build and beat your score.
  • Use community tier lists and build guides to create “rulesets” that everyone must follow.

Are There Megabonk Multiplayer Mods?

Because official online co-op doesn't exist, the community has stepped up to create unofficial multiplayer mods. The most prominent project currently is "Bonk With Friends", an ambitious mod that aims to enable true online co-op directly through Steam.

How the "Bonk With Friends" Mod Works

  • Steam P2P Lobbies: The mod utilizes Steam's peer-to-peer networking, allowing players to join lobbies and invite friends via their Steam overlay.
  • Synchronization: Current versions successfully synchronize player movements, animations, and map seeds, ensuring both players experience the exact same procedural world and enemy waves.
  • Availability: These mods are not officially supported via the Steam Workshop. Instead, they are typically distributed for free on third-party modding platforms like Thunderstore or NexusMods.

Important Modding Risks

Before downloading any multiplayer mod, keep in mind:

  • Updates Break Mods: Whenever Megabonk receives an official patch, the multiplayer mod will likely break and require updates from its creators.
  • Sync Issues: Aspects like advanced combat interactions and elite enemy tracking may sometimes desync between host and client.
  • Achievements: Using mods may disable official Steam achievements and leaderboard trackings.

If you want a flawless, 100% stable experience, stick to the vanilla single-player. But if you're willing to handle a few bugs to play with friends, the modding community has you covered.

FAQ: Common Questions About Megabonk Multiplayer

Q: Is Megabonk multiplayer?
A: No. Megabonk is currently a single-player game only. There is no official online, local, or split-screen multiplayer mode.
Q: Does Megabonk have online or couch co-op?
A: Neither is supported at the moment. You can’t invite friends into your run or play on the same screen.
Q: Are there official plans to add multiplayer later?
A: The developer has said that multiplayer is not planned right now but may be explored in the future. There is no confirmed roadmap or release date.
Q: Can I still enjoy Megabonk “with friends” somehow?
