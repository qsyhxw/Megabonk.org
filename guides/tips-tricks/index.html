Beginner Essential Tips

üí° Start Here!

If you're new to Megabonk, these fundamental tips will help you survive longer and understand core mechanics. Master these before moving to advanced strategies!

1

Start with Sir Oofie or Fox

Beginner

Sir Oofie and Fox are the two default characters for good reason. Sir Oofie gains +1% Armor per level making him extremely tanky and forgiving of mistakes. Fox gains +1% Luck per level for better item drops. Both are perfect for learning game mechanics without the pressure of complex unlock conditions.

Why this matters:
  • Sir Oofie can tank through early mistakes while you learn enemy patterns
  • Fox's luck helps you find good items faster, accelerating learning
  • Avoid unlockable characters until you understand the basics
2

Keep Moving Constantly

Beginner

Standing still = death. Megabonk's 3D movement is your greatest defensive tool. Circle strafe enemies, create distance from hordes, and never stop moving even when attacking. Most weapons auto-target, so focus on positioning rather than aiming.

Movement tips:
  • Use WASD in circular patterns around enemy groups
  • Jump over low attacks and create vertical space
  • Sprint away from large hordes to thin them out
  • Position near map edges for escape routes
3

Prioritize XP Gems Over Gold Early

Beginner

In the first 10 minutes, chase every blue XP gem you see. Leveling up gives you upgrade choices that make you stronger. Gold is important but comes naturally - XP must be actively collected. Use Magnet shrines after big fights to vacuum up all nearby gems at once.

XP optimization:
  • Clear enemies near XP gems before collecting to avoid taking damage
  • Save Magnet shrine activations for after large enemy waves
  • Don't chase gems into dangerous areas until you're stronger
  • XP gems despawn after ~30 seconds - collect important ones quickly
4

Understand the Holy Trinity (Later)

Beginner

You'll hear about XP Tome + Curse Tome + Luck Tome being mandatory. This is true for competitive play, but DON'T use this combination as a beginner. Curse Tome makes the game much harder, and you need defensive foundations first. Master basic survival before attempting Trinity runs.

When to try Holy Trinity:
  • After completing Tier 1 stages comfortably
  • Once you understand which defensive items keep you alive
  • When you can consistently reach 20+ minutes
  • See our Meta Guide for full Trinity strategies
5

Use Shrines Strategically

Beginner

Shrines offer powerful temporary or permanent bonuses. Always use Moai Shrine (free random item) and Gold Chest (guaranteed item for gold). Save Greed Shrines for when you have 100+ gold to maximize value. Avoid Curse shrines until you're comfortable with difficulty.

Essential shrines:
  • Moai Shrine: Free random item - always activate
  • Gold Chest: Spend gold for guaranteed item - worth it early
  • Magnet Shrine: Vacuums items/XP - save for after big fights
  • Greed Shrine: More gold but harder enemies - risky early game
6

Focus on One Weapon Type Early

Beginner

Don't spread upgrades across 4 different weapons. Pick your starting weapon (Sword for Sir Oofie, Firestaff for Fox) and upgrade it every chance you get for the first 10 levels. A fully upgraded weapon beats 4 weak ones. Add secondary weapons after your primary is strong.

Weapon priority:
  • Levels 1-10: Only upgrade starting weapon
  • Levels 11-20: Add one complementary weapon (Aura is universally good)
  • Levels 21+: Consider adding third weapon
  • Never take more than 3-4 weapons total
7

Watch Your Health Bar

Beginner

Sounds obvious, but in the chaos of 100+ enemies, it's easy to miss you're at 10% HP. Prioritize Health upgrades when below 50% HP. Health Pendant and HP Tome are lifesavers. Don't be greedy for damage upgrades if you keep dying - survival > damage.

Survival priorities:
  • Take HP upgrades when below 50% health
  • Health Pendant is one of the best items in the game
  • HP Regen helps but doesn't replace max HP
  • Dead = 0 DPS. Stay alive to do damage
8

Learn Enemy Attack Patterns

Beginner

Each enemy type has predictable attacks. Skeletons throw bones (dodge sideways), goblins charge in straight lines (sidestep), bats swarm from above (jump and move). Spend your first few runs just observing enemy behavior rather than focusing purely on damage.

Common enemy tells:
  • Red glow = enemy about to attack (dodge now!)
  • Charging enemies telegraph direction (step aside)
  • Flying enemies dive bomb - create distance
  • Boss wind-up animations - huge dodge window

Intermediate Strategy Tips

‚ö° Level Up Your Game

Ready to push past Tier 1? These intermediate tips focus on build optimization, item synergies, and efficient progression. Master these to consistently reach 30+ minute runs.

9

Understand Item Synergies

Intermediate

Items aren't just individual power-ups - they combo together. Kevin + Mirror creates defensive loops, Aura + Size Tome makes massive damage zones, Lifesteal + Attack Speed provides sustain. Look for multiplicative combinations rather than additive upgrades.

Top synergy combos:
  • Aura + Size + Damage: Walking death zone build
  • Revolver + Crit + Attack Speed: Glass cannon burst
  • Aegis + HP + Shield: Immortal tank
  • Kevin + Mirror + Attack Speed: Defensive loop (advanced)
  • See our Builds Guide for full synergy lists
10

Tome Priority Order

Intermediate

Not all tomes are equal. Veteran players prioritize: Luck Tome first (affects all future upgrades), then Movement Speed (survival), then Attack Speed (DPS/healing), finally Damage. Luck investment pays off exponentially as the run progresses.

Why Luck comes first:
  • Luck affects every level-up, chest, and shrine for the rest of the run
  • Better early luck = legendary items mid-game = god-tier late game
  • Movement Speed keeps you alive to use that damage
  • Attack Speed affects lifesteal, Kevin triggers, and DPS
  • Raw Damage is last because it scales linearly, not exponentially
11

Use the Microwave Item Duplicator

Intermediate

The Microwave is a map object that duplicates items. Place S-tier items like Boss Buster, Moldy Cheese, or Idle Juice in it to get duplicates. This can turn good runs into legendary ones. Microwave locations are fixed per map - learn where they spawn.

Best items to duplicate:
  • Boss Buster: +500% damage to bosses (get 2-3 copies)
  • Moldy Cheese: Poison on hit (stacks incredibly well)
  • Idle Juice: 2x damage while standing still (AFK builds)
  • Mirror: Damage reflection (multiple mirrors = constant invuln)
  • Never waste duplication on common items
12

Manage Your Item Pool

Intermediate

Don't unlock every item immediately. More items = diluted drop pool = harder to get what you need. Use the Toggler (unlocked after 40 items) to disable weak items you never want. This dramatically improves your chances of finding S-tier items.

Item pool management:
  • Only unlock items for characters you actually play
  • After 40 unlocks, Toggler lets you disable bad items
  • Disable situational items like Cactus (only good for Thorns builds)
  • Keep universal good items enabled (Aura, Aegis, Health Pendant)
  • Smaller pool = more consistent power spikes
13

Delay Opening Chests

Intermediate

Don't open chests immediately when you find them. Wait until after you've acquired Luck Tome and leveled it up. High luck dramatically increases your chances of getting Epic/Legendary items from chests. Mark chest locations and return after luck stacking.

Chest strategy:
  • Open chests only after Luck Tome level 5+
  • Gold Chests (cost gold) can be delayed indefinitely
  • Free chests have 2-3 minute despawn timers
  • Legendary items from chests can define your entire run
  • Risk vs reward: Sometimes you need immediate power
14

Embrace Curse Tome for Better Loot

Intermediate

Once you have a solid defensive foundation, Curse Tome becomes a power multiplier. More enemies = more XP gems = more levels = better items. The difficulty increase is offset by your accelerated power growth. This is why Holy Trinity works.

When to use Curse Tome:
  • After securing defensive items (Aegis, HP, Armor)
  • When your primary weapon is level 5+
  • Always pair with XP Tome for maximum leveling speed
  • Curse makes Greed Shrines actually beneficial (more enemies = more gold)
  • Late-game Curse is mandatory for 100K+ kill runs
15

Character-Specific Optimization

Intermediate

Every character has optimal builds. Don't force builds that don't fit the character's passive. CL4NK wants crit, Calcium wants speed, Megachad wants size/tank. Study character passives and build toward their strengths, not against them.

Character focus areas:
  • CL4NK: Crit Chance, Precision, Revolver synergies
  • Calcium: Movement Speed, never taking damage
  • Megachad: Size, tankiness, Aura scaling
  • Robinette: Gold generation, economic scaling
  • See our Character Guides for detailed builds
16

Learn Boss Patterns Before Final Stages

Intermediate

Don't go into Tier 2/3 blind. Each boss has specific mechanics and attack patterns. Study boss guides, watch videos, or deliberately practice in earlier tiers. Knowing when bosses telegraph attacks is the difference between deathless runs and wipes.

Boss preparation:
  • All bosses have wind-up animations before big attacks
  • Skeleton King: Dodge bone throws, clear summoned adds immediately
  • Flame Demon: Ice weapons deal +50% damage (exploit this!)
  • Storm Lord: 75%+ lightning resist required or you die instantly
  • Check our Boss Guides for full strategies

Expert-Level Techniques

‚ö†Ô∏è Advanced Players Only

These techniques require mastery of fundamentals and can be difficult to execute. They're used by top leaderboard players for 100K+ kill runs and sub-5-minute speedruns.

17

Animation Canceling for DPS

Expert

Many weapons have end-of-attack animations you can cancel by jumping, dashing, or weapon-swapping. This increases your effective DPS by 10-30%. Particularly effective with Sword, Axe, and Dexecutioner. Requires precise timing and practice.

Animation cancel techniques:
  • Jump Cancel: Jump immediately after damage frames complete
  • Dash Cancel: Sprint-dash to cut recovery frames
  • Weapon Swap: Switch weapons mid-animation (risky)
  • Practice in low-pressure situations first
  • Only worthwhile on slow weapons (Sword, Axe)
18

Kevin + Mirror Frame Perfect Setup

Expert

The Kevin + Mirror combo was nerfed but still provides near-immortality with perfect execution. Requires exactly 200-300% attack speed, Cooldown Tome, and timing Mirror's invuln frames with Kevin's self-damage. One mistake = death. Top players still use this for leaderboard pushes.

Setup requirements:
  • 200-300% attack speed (not more or less)
  • Cooldown Tome level 10+ to minimize Mirror downtime
  • Battery (+8% attack speed per level) for fine-tuning
  • Layer Aegis + HP Regen to fill gaps between procs
  • See our Kevin+Mirror Guide
19

Pixel-Perfect Positioning

Expert

Master players position within pixel-perfect ranges that maximize weapon coverage while minimizing enemy hits. For Aura builds: maintain exactly 2 character-widths from enemy clusters. For ranged: position at max range while still hitting. This requires spatial awareness that takes 50+ hours to develop.

Positioning mastery:
  • Aura optimal range: 2-3 character-widths from cluster center
  • Ranged weapons: Maximum range = zero risk, full DPS
  • Melee builds: Use hit-and-run at exact Sword/Katana range
  • Learn enemy hitbox sizes (larger than they appear)
  • Corner positioning creates predictable enemy pathing
20

Speedrun Route Optimization

Expert

Speedrunners have mapped optimal routes for Tier 1-3 completion. Know exactly where every Moai Shrine, Gold Chest, and Microwave spawns. Pre-plan your build path: first 5 minutes XP farming, 5-15 minutes item collection, 15+ minutes boss rush. World record holders finish Tier 1 in under 2 minutes.

Speedrun principles:
  • Skip all non-essential fights - beeline for objectives
  • Monke dominates speedruns (wall-climbing shortcuts)
  • Dexecutioner execute mechanic = instant boss phases
  • Ignore Holy Trinity completely - raw damage only
  • Current records: T1 in 45s, T2 in 4m58s (by GoGeng)
21

Pattern Recognition & Prediction

Expert

Expert players don't react to attacks - they predict them. Enemy spawn patterns are semi-random but follow rules. After 100+ hours, you'll subconsciously know when swarms spawn, where bosses will telegraph attacks, and which direction enemies will move. This "game sense" is what separates top 100 players.

Pattern recognition skills:
  • Enemy spawn waves follow 30-second intervals
  • Boss attacks have fixed rotation orders (learn the sequence)
  • Elite enemies (glowing) always spawn at minute markers
  • Shrine spawns are fixed per map seed (memorizable)
  • Predict rather than react = 10x faster responses
22

Advanced Item Duplication Chains

Expert

Microwave duplication can be chained across multiple maps if done correctly. Duplicate Boss Buster 3 times in Forest, carry duplicates into Desert, duplicate again. By Tier 3, you can have 6+ copies of S-tier items. Requires precise map knowledge and timing.

Duplication chains:
  • Each map has 1-2 Microwave spawns
  • Items persist between stages (exploit this)
  • Priority: Boss Buster > Moldy Cheese > Idle Juice
  • 6 Boss Busters = +3000% boss damage (one-shot everything)
  • Map Microwave locations in Practice Mode first
23

Meta-Breaking Combo Discoveries

Expert

The community constantly finds new broken combinations. Current meta-breaking combos: Dice + Giant Fork (Megacrit), Joe's Dagger stacking (capped now but still strong), Golden Shield + Kevin (infinite gold), Athena low-HP + Kevin (controlled risk). Experiment in Practice Mode to find next-gen strategies before they're patched.

Experimental combos to try:
  • Multiple Thorns items + low HP = enemy suicide
  • Max Cooldown Reduction + short-cooldown weapons = spam
  • Extreme Size + Aura + Agility = mobile death zone
  • Lifesteal + Attack Speed + Damage = immortal DPS
  • Join Discord to discuss discoveries

Essential Pro Tips

üéØ Always Use Greed Shrines with Curse

The "make game harder for more gold" shrines seem risky, but with Curse Tome active, the difficulty is already maxed. Free gold with no downside. Pro players activate every Greed Shrine they find post-Curse.

üîÑ Restart if No Good Items by 5 Minutes

Top players restart if they don't find XP/Luck/Curse Tome by minute 5, or get terrible weapon RNG. Time investment in bad runs is wasted - better to restart and get good RNG. Ruthless but effective for leaderboard pushes.

üìä Prioritize Compound Scaling

+10% damage now vs +1% luck per level? Take luck. Immediate power feels good but exponential scaling wins 30+ minute runs. This is why Holy Trinity dominates - it's all compound scaling.

üéÆ Jump While Attacking

Jumping while attacking gives brief invulnerability to ground-based attacks. Megachad can literally jump constantly while using Aura to damage enemies below while taking zero hits. Master this for tank builds.

üî• Don't Sleep on Flamewalker

Underrated S-tier weapon. Creates fire trails as you move - pairs perfectly with high movement speed builds. Calcium + Flamewalker is a top-tier speedrun combo. Free damage just from positioning.

üíæ Save After Every Good Run

Game autosaves but manual saves prevent losing progress to crashes. After unlocking key items/characters, immediately exit to menu and let it save. Don't risk 2-hour runs to crashes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

‚ùå Mistake #1: Ignoring Defense for Pure Damage

New players stack damage upgrades and wonder why they die at minute 12. Dead = 0 DPS. You need survivability to reach the point where damage scaling matters. Balance offense and defense in the first 20 minutes.

‚úÖ Solution:

Take at least 2-3 defensive upgrades (HP, Armor, Shield) in the first 10 levels. Aegis Shield is the single best defensive item. Health Pendant is S-tier. Prioritize not dying over killing faster.

‚ùå Mistake #2: Standing Still While Attacking

Megabonk isn't a bullet hell where you dodge skillshots while stationary. It's 3D - you have infinite space to maneuver. Standing still to "aim better" gets you surrounded and killed. Most weapons auto-target anyway.

‚úÖ Solution:

Circle strafe constantly. Even while "focusing damage," maintain circular movement around enemy groups. Only exception: AFK Megachad builds with Idle Juice (intentionally stationary).

‚ùå Mistake #3: Unlocking Too Many Items Early

Every item you unlock dilutes the item pool. Unlocking all 70+ items means finding the items you actually need becomes nearly impossible. New players unlock everything then complain about bad RNG.

‚úÖ Solution:

Only unlock items for characters you actively play. After 40 unlocks, use Toggler to disable situational items. Keep pool focused on universally good items (Aura, Aegis, Luck Tome, Health Pendant).

‚ùå Mistake #4: Neglecting Movement Speed

Slow characters are dead characters. Even tank builds need movement to reposition. Ogre and Athena's low movement speed is their biggest weakness. Movement Speed upgrades are undervalued by beginners.

‚úÖ Solution:

Take Agility Tome for slow characters (Ogre, Sir Oofie, Athena). Turbo Socks/Skates are S-tier items. Movement = survival = more time dealing damage. Especially critical for Curse Tome runs.

‚ùå Mistake #5: Not Using Shrines Strategically

Players either activate every shrine immediately or ignore them entirely. Both are wrong. Shrines have optimal timing windows that maximize value. Random activation wastes their potential.

‚úÖ Solution:

Moai Shrine: Always use immediately (free item). Gold Chest: Use when you have 100+ gold and high Luck. Magnet: Save for after big fights. Greed: Only with Curse Tome active. Curse Shrine: Never use early game.

‚ùå Mistake #6: Spreading Weapon Upgrades Too Thin

Taking 5 different weapons and upgrading them all equally means you have 5 weak weapons. Focus fire on 2-3 weapons maximum. A level 10 weapon beats two level 5 weapons every time.

‚úÖ Solution:

First 10 levels: Only upgrade starting weapon. Levels 11-20: Add one complementary weapon. Levels 21+: Maybe add third weapon. Never take more than 3-4 weapons total. Quality over quantity.

‚ùå Mistake #7: Playing Without Specific Goals

Wandering around randomly, taking whatever upgrades appear, no plan for your build. This wastes runs. Top players enter every run with a specific strategy and win condition in mind.

‚úÖ Solution:

Before starting: Decide if you're doing Holy Trinity run, speedrun, or specific challenge. Know which items you need to find. Have backup plans for bad RNG. Study character-specific builds beforehand.

‚ùå Mistake #8: Attempting Holy Trinity Too Early

Beginners hear "XP + Curse + Luck is mandatory" and try it immediately, then die at minute 8 and think the game is impossible. Curse Tome makes difficulty spike dramatically - you need foundations first.

‚úÖ Solution:

Master basic survival on Sir Oofie first. Complete Tier 1 comfortably without Holy Trinity. Understand which defensive items keep you alive. Only then attempt Curse Tome runs. Holy Trinity is endgame strategy, not beginner advice.
