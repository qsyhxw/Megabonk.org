Quick Answer

Shrines are randomly distributed stage interactables. Charge Shrines trade time and positioning for a stat choice; Greed Shrines add 5% Difficulty for an economic reward; Boss Curse adds another boss copy and another chest if you win. Identify the type first, then compare its reward with your build's current margin.

How Charge Shrines Work

Interaction

Hold the circle

Stand within the Shrine's radius until the charging action completes. Enter with enough space, defense, or crowd control to avoid being forced out.

Reward

Choose one of three stats

After a regular Charge Shrine completes, select one of three offered stat upgrades. Pick the stat that fixes the build's current bottleneck rather than the one with the flashiest number.

Rare variant

Golden Charge Shrine

The Golden version uses the same charging idea but offers legendary stat upgrades, making a safe completion especially valuable.

Safe setup

Clear, then commit

Approach with cooldowns ready, clear immediate pressure, and position the character so persistent or area damage continues while charging.

How Greed Shrines Work

A Greed Shrine is a permanent risk decision for the run: each activation adds 5% Difficulty and gives an economic reward. The important question is not whether more currency is useful; it is whether the build can still clear the harder run after taking it.

Take

Build is ahead

Ordinary waves disappear quickly, boss damage is strong, and survival has room for error.

Skip

Build is behind

Enemies already survive too long, movement is mostly retreat, or the current boss is a serious threat.

Recheck

After major upgrades

A Greed Shrine that was unsafe earlier may become reasonable after a chest, stat spike, or completed Charge Shrine.

Do not judge Greed only by the current wave. The extra Difficulty also matters when the stage reaches its boss and dense late pressure.

How Boss Curse Works

Boss Curse adds another boss copy to the encounter. Beating the extra boss creates another chest, so the altar converts boss-fight difficulty into more loot.

  1. Judge the normal boss first

    If one copy already takes too long or forces repeated mistakes, do not multiply the problem.

  2. Check overlapping attacks

    Extra copies can make otherwise manageable patterns arrive together. Defense and mobility matter as much as raw damage.

  3. Match the activation to the goal

    Use one activation for optional extra loot only when safe. For the Speed Boi unlock, the target is clearing a stage with two or more Boss Curses active.

  4. Protect the clear

    The reward matters only if the stage is completed. Stop stacking once the next copy threatens the run's real objective.

For boss-specific loadouts and detailed combat preparation, continue to the Boss Curse guide.

Shrine Map Distribution & Routing

Shrines are placed randomly around stages. A route should therefore search efficiently without assuming fixed coordinates, a guaranteed order, or a universal Shrine count.

Use wide scouting loops

Explore new ground while maintaining enough access to XP and enemies. Repeatedly crossing cleared space costs time without improving Shrine discovery.

Do not activate immediately

Mark a risky Shrine and return after the build improves. This is especially useful for Greed, Boss Curse, or a Charge Shrine in a dangerous area.

Plan for weather unlocks

On Desert, keep an unused Charge Shrine available when working on the Tornado requirement during Sandstorms.

Separate general and special interactables

Some statue-like encounters have map-specific behavior. For example, the Bandit Statue is associated with Desert, but it is not the same decision as Charge, Greed, or Boss Curse.

No fixed-coordinate promise: use the visible stage layout and exploration loop. Exact positions and counts should not be assumed to carry between runs.

Beacon and Wrench

Rare item

Beacon

Completing a Charge Shrine creates a healing zone. Beacon also adds two more Charge Shrines to future stages per copy, increasing future stat-upgrade opportunities.

Choose it when: the run expects to continue through later stages and can consistently finish Charge Shrines.

Common item

Wrench

Shrines charge four seconds faster and grant 7.5% more Shrine rewards. Its value rises with the number of Shrines you can safely complete.

Choose it when: routing and charging are already part of the run plan.

Other Common Shrine Interactions

Challenge Shrine

Starts a combat challenge. Defeat the summoned enemies to earn a chest.

Shrine of Succ

Pulls uncollected XP toward the player, making it useful after a wide farming loop.

Moai Shrine

Provides a selection of power-up or item rewards without the same combat risk as Boss Curse.

Common Shrine Mistakes

Charging under full pressure

Clear nearby threats and prepare persistent damage before standing in the Charge Shrine radius.

Taking Greed while behind

Currency does not repair a run if the additional Difficulty causes the build to collapse before it can spend the reward.

Stacking Boss Curse for pride

Extra chests are irrelevant after a failed stage. Stop at the number required by the run goal.

Breaking an unlock route

Using every Charge Shrine immediately can remove the weather window needed for Tornado or make a full-map completion harder to audit.

Shrines FAQ

What does a Charge Shrine do?

Remain inside its radius until the charge finishes, then choose one of three stat upgrades. Golden Charge Shrines offer legendary stat upgrades.

What does a Greed Shrine do?

It adds 5% Difficulty per activation and provides an economic reward. Take it only when the build has enough clear and boss margin for the harder run.

Does Boss Curse give better rewards?

It adds another boss copy; defeating that additional boss creates another chest. The reward is conditional on surviving the harder encounter.

Are Shrine locations fixed?

No reliable fixed coordinates should be assumed. Shrines are distributed randomly, so route through unexplored parts of the stage.

Does Wrench affect Shrine rewards?

Yes. Wrench makes Shrines charge four seconds faster and grants 7.5% more Shrine rewards.

How does Beacon change Charge Shrines?

Completing a Charge Shrine creates a healing zone, and each Beacon adds two more Charge Shrines to future stages.
