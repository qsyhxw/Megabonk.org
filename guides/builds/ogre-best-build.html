💥 S-TIER BUILD

Ogre Pure Berserker Build

Wide cleaves, stable close-range coverage, and safe chain control.

Primary Style: Melee cleave (aggressive)
Difficulty: Beginner-friendly positioning
Power Curve: Strong early → snowballs on scaling

⚔️ Core Weapons Setup

Use a wide-cleave core with passive coverage and a safe ranged chainer.

Axe (core)

Aura (passive AoE)

Lightning Staff (chains)

Sword (alt melee)

Axe

CORE

Broad cleaving arcs that reliably hit multiple targets. Scales excellently with Damage, Quantity, and Size. Not the single highest base damage in the game, but its coverage + scaling makes it a premier Ogre core.

Aura

COVERAGE

Passive close-range AoE that constantly chips enemies who enter melee. Pairs perfectly with Axe to clean up small foes and protect your space. Scales with Size and Cooldown.

Lightning Staff

CHAIN

Safe, bouncing chains that thin out packs and pick off ranged threats. For reliable stuns, add the Lightning Orb item—Lightning Staff itself focuses on chaining.

Sword (Alternative)

ALT

Consistent close-range slashes. Use if you prefer active swings over passive coverage; still scales on Damage, Cooldown, and Quantity.

⏱️ Practical Progression

Prioritize upgrades based on what the shop offers; don’t force a fixed script.

0–3

Get Online

Look for Axe and a coverage tool (preferably Aura). Pick early economy tomes if they show without skipping strong combat tomes.

3–7

Scale Damage & Frequency

Start stacking Damage, Quantity, and Cooldown. Add Lightning Staff as ranged chainer.

7–12

Lock Stability

Grow Size for much larger Axe arcs & Aura radius. Slot in items like Gym Sauce / Demonic Soul and Mirror or Chonkplate for comfort.

12+

Snowball

Continue stacking your best tomes and fill remaining slots. If you unlocked more weapon slots, consider a fourth slot only when it truly complements your scaling.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Treating Power Gloves as “attack speed”

They create on-hit explosions. For faster swings, use Cooldown Tome (and items like Battery if available).

❌ Locking into a fixed 3-weapon rule

Aim for a focused set of 3–4 depending on your unlocks. A tight kit often outperforms unfocused 4-slot setups.

❌ Confusing “stun” source

Lightning Staff chains; stuns come from Lightning Orb. Combine them for control.

❌ Overvaluing Size before Frequency

Size is great, but Quantity and Cooldown often increase real DPS earlier.

❌ Expecting Gym Sauce to “scale over time”

It’s a reliable flat damage boost—still excellent, just not a ramping effect on its own.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What’s the safest 3-weapon core?

Axe + Aura + Lightning Staff covers melee cleave, passive protection, and safe ranged chaining. If you unlock a 4th slot, add a comfort pick like Sword or a utility weapon.

Do I always open with XP → Luck → Curse?

They are excellent early options when offered, but not mandatory. If strong combat tomes appear early, it’s fine to take those first.

Best defensive choice?

Mirror for projectile matchups; Shield Tome when learning melee spacing; Aegis (weapon) if you prefer active blocks and shockwaves; Chonkplate if you lean into sustain.

Tip: Ogre excels when you stack Frequency (Cooldown/Quantity) and Damage while keeping a steady coverage layer (Aura/Lightning Staff). Use the Lightning Orb if you want consistent stuns, and don’t forget to add at least one defensive tool (Mirror, Shield Tome, or Aegis).
