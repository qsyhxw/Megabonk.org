How to Kill Spooky Steve in Megabonk

Trigger him in the right Crypt, build around percentage-based boss damage, keep him controlled, and finish the fight without letting one close-range hit end the run.

Reviewed July 24, 2026

Quick answer

Do not fight Spooky Steve in the starting Crypt. Escape, collect four Crypt Keys, power up around the Graveyard, then enter the second Crypt. Let its timer expire, use the short grace window to position, and kill him with repeated Dexecutioner execute procs while long-duration crowd control keeps him away. Athena with Aegis is the safest setup; defeating Steve unlocks Roberto.

Where and How Spooky Steve Spawns

Map

Graveyard Crypt

Steve can appear in either timed Crypt. The second Crypt is the practical target because the outdoor section gives you time to finish the build.

Trigger

Let the timer expire

When the displayed Crypt timer reaches zero, a short grace window remains before Steve appears. Zero is your warning to take position.

Second Crypt access

Collect four keys

Find the four marked coffins outside, defeat their guardians, pick up every Crypt Key, and use the complete set at the Crypt Door.

Reward

Roberto unlock

Defeating Spooky Steve completes Roberto's boss requirement and makes the character available for purchase.

The first Crypt is a trap for this objective: Steve can spawn there, but your weapons and tomes will normally be too weak. Leave first; fight later.

Spooky Steve Mechanics and Fight Stages

Spooky Steve does not use a conventional health-threshold phase change. The encounter becomes harder because his enormous health pool punishes flat-damage builds, his close-range hits can end a run, and repeated short control effects become less dependable if the fight lasts too long.

Fight stateWhat happensYour response
Spawn warningThe Crypt timer reaches zero, followed by a brief grace window.Stop looting, move away from walls and the exit edge, and make sure your defensive charges are ready.

Extended fightShort repeated stuns and freezes may stop holding him reliably; contact becomes the main danger.Favor long-duration control, preserve open floor behind you, and finish with execute procs before he reaches you repeatedly.

Why normal DPS looks broken: the displayed health pool is roughly 340.3 Ud. Ordinary damage can be working while barely moving the bar. Dexecutioner's boss interaction is valuable because its execute proc removes a percentage-sized chunk instead of relying only on flat damage.

Best Spooky Steve Build

The safest reproducible plan uses Athena for full-hit protection through her Aegis interaction, Dexecutioner as the real boss killer, and long crowd-control windows. The build is designed around more execute attempts, not a giant displayed Damage stat.

Step-by-Step Kill Route

1

Escape the opening Crypt

Take only safe pickups on the way out. Do not wait for Steve during the low-level opening.

2

Build outside

Find all four key coffins, defeat the guardians, and level Dexecutioner, control weapons, and core tomes.

3

Run a readiness check

Confirm four keys, reliable ordinary-enemy clear, strong Duration, frequent Dexecutioner hits, and at least one defensive plan.

4

Enter the second Crypt

Use the keys, advance through the interior, then choose an open area where you are not pinned against a wall or doorway.

5

Wait through the warning

When the timer reaches zero, stop opening distant loot. Let Aegis recharge and use the grace window to create space.

6

Control, hit, reposition

Keep Black Hole and Frostwalker between you and Steve, maintain Dexecutioner contact, and move only enough to preserve open floor.

Pre-fight checklist

  • Four Crypt Keys collected and the second Crypt opened.
  • Dexecutioner obtained and upgraded for frequent hits.
  • Duration is high enough that control effects overlap.
  • Aegis charges or another survival window are ready.
  • You are standing away from walls, corners, and map edges.
  • Ordinary enemies are not overwhelming the arena around Steve.

Why Spooky Steve Kills the Run

Wrong timing

Fighting in the first Crypt

You have not had time to build Duration, control, defense, or enough execute attempts. Escape and return stronger.

Wrong damage plan

Stacking only flat Damage

A huge number on the stat screen is not the same as percentage-based boss damage. Bring Dexecutioner.

Control gap

Too many short effects

A pile of rapid short stuns may become unreliable before Steve dies. Longer windows and continuous execute pressure are safer.

Bad arena position

Starting against a wall

Steve can close the distance and remove your escape lane. Use the grace window to take the center or another open route.

False bug report

The health bar barely moves

This usually means the build is relying on ordinary damage. Watch for large execute chunks rather than small continuous ticks.

Greedy routing

Looting after zero

The displayed timer is not the instant spawn, but the remaining grace period is short. Position first; treasure comes second.

Spooky Steve FAQ

Where does Spooky Steve spawn?

He appears inside the first or second Crypt on the Graveyard map shortly after the Crypt timer reaches zero. The second Crypt is the better place to fight because you can build outside first.

What is the easiest way to kill Spooky Steve?

Use Athena with Aegis, Dexecutioner, Black Hole, Frostwalker, and high Duration. Keep Steve controlled while repeated Dexecutioner hits trigger percentage-based boss damage.

Do I need Dexecutioner?

It is the most reliable option. Flat damage is impractical against Steve's health pool, while Dexecutioner's boss execute interaction removes meaningful chunks. Joe's Dagger can help if it drops, but do not plan the route around getting it.

Why does Spooky Steve stop staying stunned?

In a long fight, repeated short control effects become less dependable. Increase Duration, keep control windows overlapping, and maintain execute pressure so the fight ends before Steve reaches you repeatedly.

What do you get for killing Spooky Steve?

You complete the boss requirement for Roberto and unlock Roberto for purchase from the character selection screen.

Can Spooky Steve spawn after the timer reaches zero?

Yes. Zero starts a short grace window rather than an instant failure. Use that brief delay to stop looting, recharge defenses, and move into an open position.
