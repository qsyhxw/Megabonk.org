🔍 Weapon Overview

Shotgun is a manual-aim spread weapon that fires multiple pellets in a cone toward your cursor. It excels at close range when several pellets hit the same target, and falls off at distance as the spread widens.

📊 Community Tier

A–S (varies by list) — Frequently rated among top weapons for burst at close range.

🔓 How to Unlock

~5% chance from breaking Tumbleweeds on Desert Stage 2 (RNG event). After it drops, claim it in Unlocks/Shop.

💰 Purchase

Buy with Silver in the Unlocks tab after the drop is obtained (in-game cost shown on unlock screen).

🎯 Targeting

Manual Aim — Direction follows your mouse cursor.

⚙️ Core Mechanics

💥 Spread & Pellets

Each shot releases multiple pellets in a cone. Several pellets can hit the same enemy for heavy burst. Damage reliability scales with proximity: the closer you are, the more pellets connect.

🎯 Manual Aiming

Unlike auto-targeting options (e.g., Aura/Katana), Shotgun requires cursor aim. Players comfortable with mouse aiming can leverage precise positioning for huge payoffs.

📈 Upgrades That Matter Most

  • Projectile Count — More pellets per shot = higher burst & better coverage.
  • Cooldown/Attack Speed — More frequent blasts; scales particularly well with certain characters.
  • Damage — Buffs every pellet.
  • Size — Slightly more forgiving pellet hitboxes and coverage.
  • Projectile Speed — Lower priority (the weapon shines up close).

⚠️ Priority Tip

Stack Projectile Count first, then Cooldown/Attack Speed. These multiply the weapon’s effectiveness far more than other stats.

🔓 Unlocking Shotgun (RNG)

  1. Enter Desert Stage 2

    Only Stage 2 Tumbleweeds can drop Shotgun. (Stage 1 Tumbleweeds are for Katana.)

  2. Find & Break Tumbleweeds

    Tumbleweeds roll around the map. Chase them and interact/attack to break them.

  3. ~5% Drop Chance

    Community and guides list ~1 in 20 odds per Tumbleweed. Actual results vary with RNG.

  4. No empty slot required

    The unlock is granted when it drops; you don’t need a free weapon slot in the run.

  5. Buy in Unlocks/Shop

    After the drop is obtained, purchase it with Silver in the Unlocks tab to add it permanently to your pool.

💡 Farming Tips

  • Pick movement speed upgrades (e.g., Turbo Socks +15% Move Speed) for easier chasing.
  • Play on a difficulty you can clear comfortably to maximize Tumbleweed checks.
  • Expect multiple runs; drop odds are low but consistent over time.

Passive: +1% Critical Chance per level. Every pellet can crit, so Quantity + crit items scale hard.

Helpful Items: Forbidden Juice (+10% Crit Chance), Giant Fork (crits can “Megacrit”), Overpowered Lamp (+1 extra proc roll for on-hit effects), Backpack.

Passive: +1% Armor per level. Makes point-blank positioning safer while you land full pellet cones.

Suggested Tomes: Armor/HP (for comfort), Quantity, Cooldown

Helpful Items: Oats (+25 Max HP), Medkit (+45 HP Regen), Mirror (reflect & brief i-frames), Boss Buster.

⚔️ Combat Strategy & Positioning

🎯 Range Management

  • Point-Blank: Maximum burst (most pellets connect).
  • Close: Good cone coverage; safer than point-blank.
  • Mid: Fewer pellets land; use backup weapons.
  • Long: Inefficient due to spread.

    • 🏃 Movement Patterns

    ✅ Circle-Strafe at Close Range

    Circle around packs or bosses while blasting into the center. Keeps enemies clustered so more pellets hit.

    🧩 Terrain Usage

    • Funnel: Use obstacles to create choke points and fire into the crowd.
    • Corners: Limit approach angles; tighten enemy groupings.
    • Loops: Run repeat loops through packs for drive-by blasts.

    ⚖️ Strengths & Weaknesses

    ✅ Strengths

    • High burst when multiple pellets connect
    • Excellent crowd delete at close range with Quantity scaling
    • Pairs well with attack speed & crit passives
    • On-hit items benefit from many pellet rolls (with Lamp, etc.)

    ❌ Weaknesses

    • Manual aim & close-range risk
    • Damage falls off sharply at long range
    • RNG unlock (~5%) can take several runs
