Credit Card (Green)

⭐⭐ RARE ITEM
Effect: Gain +2% Luck each time you open a chest (applies for the current run).
📈 Long-Term Investment

🧪 How Credit Card (Green) Works

Credit Card (Green) is a Rare passive Item. Every chest you open adds +2% Luck for the rest of that run. This applies to regular, free, and golden chests.

Mechanics Breakdown

  • Triggers on any chest: Includes free and golden chests.
  • Run-scoped: The Luck bonus resets when the run ends.
  • Scaling: Stacks with each chest; players report reaching very high Luck in long runs.
  • What Luck does: Improves the odds of higher-tier loot and upgrades during the run.
🔬 Technical Note

The +2% Luck stacks additively with other Luck sources (e.g., Clover, tomes), enhancing your overall chance to see Rare/Epic/Legendary rewards.

Why Use It?

It’s a classic scale-now, snowball-later item. Early pickups can dramatically improve your item quality by mid-/late-game.

✓ Advantages

  • Strong long-term scaling
  • Significantly improves loot quality over time
  • Passive—no activation required
  • Great in full-clear/chest-farm routes

✗ Disadvantages

  • No immediate combat stats
  • Much weaker if found late
  • Relies on chest RNG

🔓 How to Unlock Credit Card (Green)

Unlock Requirement

Available by default.

  1. No Unlock Needed
    It’s in the base item pool from your first run.
  2. Find in chests or as rewards
    Appears at lower odds than commons due to its Rare tier.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How does Credit Card (Green) work in Megabonk?
Each time you open any chest (including free or golden), it adds +2% Luck for the rest of the run.
Q: Is the +2% Luck permanent forever?
No. It’s permanent for the current run only.
Q: What does Luck do?
Luck improves the odds of seeing higher-tier rewards from chests and upgrade choices during a run.
Q: How is it different from Credit Card (Red)?
Red Card adds +2.5% Damage per chest—more immediate power, less long-term loot scaling.
Q: Is Credit Card (Green) good?
Excellent if found early; mediocre when found late. Value tracks how many chests you can still open.
