🎯 Weapon Overview

⚠️ Honest Community Assessment

Chunkers is widely considered one of the weaker weapons in Megabonk's arsenal. Steam community discussions frequently describe it as a "dud weapon" that "needs so much investment compared to other weapons." While it has a respectable 12 base damage (highest among default weapons), its orbital mechanic and knockback create fundamental problems that hold it back from higher tier rankings.

The Chunkers is a default unlocked orbital weapon that summons chunky rocks to orbit around the player, dealing damage and knocking back any enemies they contact. During their orbit duration, the rocks slowly move away from the player until they disappear and their cycle resets. On paper, this creates a defensive barrier of rotating projectiles—in practice, the knockback mechanic often works against you.

⚙️ Weapon Mechanics

Core Mechanics:

  • 🪨 Orbital Projectiles: Rocks spawn and orbit around your character
  • 💫 Gradual Expansion: Rocks slowly drift away from player during orbit
  • 🔄 Cycle Reset: After reaching maximum distance, rocks disappear and respawn
  • 👊 Knockback on Hit: Rocks push enemies away on contact
  • 🎯 Close-Range Requirement: Most effective when near enemies

The Knockback Problem: While knockback sounds defensive, it creates a paradox—Chunkers requires close-range positioning to hit enemies, but the knockback pushes enemies away, reducing your damage uptime. This contradictory design is Chunkers' fundamental weakness and why weapons like Aura (same range requirement, no knockback) significantly outperform it.

📊 Upgrade Priorities

Priority Upgrade Type Impact Reasoning
1️⃣ Highest Size ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Larger rocks = better coverage and more hits before knockback
2️⃣ High Projectile Count ⭐⭐⭐⭐ More rocks = better orbital coverage, but dilutes scaling
3️⃣ Medium Damage ⭐⭐⭐ Direct damage increase, always useful
4️⃣ Low Projectile Speed ⭐⭐ Faster orbit = more hits, but faster knockback timing issues
5️⃣ Situational Knockback ⭐ Actually hurts effectiveness—avoid unless building crowd control

Five Upgrade Stats = Diluted Scaling: Chunkers has five possible upgrade stats (Damage, Knockback, Projectile Count, Projectile Speed, Size), which means when you level up, you're less likely to get the specific upgrades you need. Contrast this with weapons like Aura (only 3 stats) or Revolver (focused stats)—fewer upgrade options means more consistent power scaling.

✓✗ Pros and Cons

✓ Strengths

  • High Base Damage: 12 base damage is excellent (highest among default weapons)
  • Default Unlock: Available immediately, no quest requirements
  • 360° Coverage: Rocks orbit around you providing all-direction defense
  • Passive Defense: Works automatically while you focus on movement
  • Crowd Control Potential: Knockback can create breathing room
  • Size Scaling: With Size Tome, becomes more effective

✗ Weaknesses

  • Knockback Paradox: Pushes enemies away, reducing damage uptime
  • Close-Range Requirement: Risky positioning in swarms
  • Five Upgrade Stats: Diluted scaling compared to focused weapons
  • Gradual Expansion: Rocks drift away, reducing coverage
  • Outclassed by Aura: Same orbital concept, but Aura is simply better
  • High Investment Needed: Requires Size Tome to reach "decent" status
  • Community Consensus: C-tier weapon that struggles to compete

No Significant Changes: Unlike weapons like Axe, Sword, and Dexecutioner which received major buffs from the Duration bug fix, Chunkers remains unchanged. Its C-tier status persists.

Community Call for Buffs

Steam community discussions consistently request buffs for Chunkers. Suggested improvements include:

  • Remove or reduce knockback - The core problem
  • Increase base damage to 15+ - Compensate for knockback issues
  • Reduce upgrade stat count to 3 - Focus scaling like Aura
  • Add Duration scaling - Similar to Axe's viable mechanic

💡 Pro Tips (What Little There Are)

  • 1. If Using Chunkers, Prioritize Size: The one stat that meaningfully improves effectiveness is Size. Always choose Size upgrades.
  • 2. Pair with Tank Characters: Sir Oofie and Megachad can survive close-range positioning better, making Chunkers' requirements less punishing.
  • 3. Use as Third/Fourth Weapon Only: Never make Chunkers your primary damage source. It's a supplemental defensive weapon at best.
  • 4. Consider Toggling Off After Unlocking Others: Once you unlock better weapons, use the Toggler to disable Chunkers from appearing in runs.
  • 5. Accept Knockback's Double Edge: In very crowded situations, the knockback can create breathing room. In most situations, it reduces your damage.
  • 6. Don't Over-Invest: The biggest trap is thinking "maybe with enough upgrades..." No. The weapon's design is flawed. Move on to better options.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Chunkers worth using?

A: No, not really. While it has the highest base damage among default weapons (12), the knockback mechanic and five upgrade stats make it significantly worse than alternatives like Aura, Axe, or even Bone. Use it only if you're testing builds or doing challenges.

Q: How can I make Chunkers viable?

A: Stack Size Tome to maximum and prioritize Size on every Chunkers upgrade. This makes the rocks large enough to provide meaningful coverage. However, even fully optimized Chunkers underperforms compared to meta weapons with no optimization.

Q: Why is Chunkers C-tier when it has 12 base damage?

A: Base damage doesn't tell the whole story. The knockback mechanic pushes enemies away, reducing damage uptime. Five upgrade stats dilute scaling. Close-range requirement is risky. Aura has only 8 base damage but is S-tier because it lacks these fundamental problems.

Q: Will Chunkers get buffed in future patches?

A: Unknown, but the community is vocal about wanting improvements. The developer (vedinad) has shown willingness to balance weapons based on feedback, so buffs are possible in future versions.

Q: What characters work best with Chunkers?

A: Tank characters like Sir Oofie (armor stacking) and Megachad (damage immunity) can survive close-range positioning better. However, even they have better weapon options available.

🎯 Final Verdict

C-Tier: Avoid Unless Testing

Chunkers is a well-intentioned weapon hampered by contradictory design. The orbital rock concept provides 360° coverage, but the knockback mechanic fundamentally undermines effectiveness by pushing enemies away from your damage zone. With five upgrade stats diluting power scaling and requiring massive Size investment to reach "decent" status, Chunkers cannot compete with meta weapons.

The community consensus is clear: Chunkers needs buffs. Steam discussions describe it as a "dud weapon" that "needs so much investment" to be viable. When weapons like Aura provide superior performance with the same orbital concept and no knockback issues, there's simply no compelling reason to use Chunkers beyond curiosity or challenge runs.

Recommendation: Use Aura instead. If you want melee orbitals, use Axe. If you want default weapons, use Firestaff, Lightning Staff, or Sword. Chunkers might receive buffs in future patches—until then, it remains one of Megabonk's weakest weapons despite promising base stats.
