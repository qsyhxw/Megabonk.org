⏱️ Minute-by-Minute Progression

A practical route—adapt to drops, elite waves, and patch notes.

0–3

🌱 Early Setup

Look for Sword first. Consider the community opener XP → Luck → Cursed if pacing feels safe; otherwise take Size immediately. Grab Idle Juice whenever it appears.

3–7

🛡️ Fortress Online

Add Aegis. Stack Size and the first Quantity. If offered, pick Battery for smoother swings and Mirror for survivability.

7–12

⚡ Stabilize & Scale

Pick Aura, then add Quantity and Damage. Slot Shield if you’re eating chip hits. With Idle Juice running, most waves melt as they reach you.

12–18

🔥 Ramp

Take Demonic Soul for per-kill scaling; Chonkplate if you want sustain. Add some Cooldown stacks once coverage is large enough.

18+

🏆 Hold the Line

Maintain position in open space; micro-adjust for bosses and projectile walls. Continue upgrading your preferred tomes and on-hit items.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ Moving Too Much

Your damage depends on standing. Reposition only for safety/boss telegraphs.

❌ Skipping Size

Size is core to the fortress concept—coverage first, DPS second.

❌ Mislabeling Items

Battery = attack speed. Power Gloves/Thunder Mitts = on-hit effects. Time Bracelet = XP. Golden Shield = economy.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Best weapon trio?

Sword + Aegis + Aura. They cover offense, defense, and constant AoE.

Is Mirror real “invincibility”?

Mirror reflects and grants brief i-frames. It’s not permanent invulnerability, but it saves you from bursts and projectile walls.

How many weapons should I take?

This build is tuned around three slots (Sword/Aegis/Aura) to maximize Tome upgrades. You can flex a fourth in some patches, but expect slower scaling on your core trio.

Patch note tip: If a patch lowers early XP value or increases enemy pressure, open with Size and survivability before committing to the classic XP → Luck → Cursed sequence.
