Damage Brackets in Megabonk — How Multipliers Really Stack

In Megabonk, damage bonuses don't simply "add together." They are grouped into brackets: bonuses within the same bracket combine additively, but different brackets multiply with each other. Understanding this system is the key to massive DPS.

⚔️ GAME MECHANICS • DAMAGE FORMULA

📐 The Core Rule

Megabonk's damage system uses 8 independent brackets. The rule is simple:

  • Inside the same bracket → bonuses add together (additive, linear scaling)
  • Between different brackets → totals multiply each other (multiplicative)
Damage Formula (Simplified)
Final Damage = Base
× (1 + Tome)
× (1 + Noelle)
× (1 + Flat Damage Items)
× (1 + Main Damage %)
× (1 + Speedy Boi)
× (1 + Elite Damage)
× (1 + Corrupted Sword)
× Giant Fork Megacrit
Each line is a separate bracket. Items within each bracket add together first, then all brackets multiply.
💡 Why This Matters

Having +50% in Bracket A and +50% in Bracket B gives 1.5 × 1.5 = 2.25× total damage. But stacking +100% in just one bracket gives only 1 + 1.0 = 2.0×. Spreading bonuses across brackets always wins.

⚠️ The Main Bracket Is Crowded

Bracket 4 (Main "Damage %") has 11 items — the most of any bracket. Because items within the same bracket only add together, stacking many items in this bracket has diminishing returns relative to investing in other brackets. A single item in a solo bracket (like Speedy Boi or Giant Fork) can be more impactful than adding another item to the main bracket.

🧮 Worked Example: Multi-Bracket vs Single-Bracket

Let's compare two scenarios to show why bracket diversity matters:

✅ Scenario A: Spread Across 3 Brackets

Base Damage: 100
Bracket 3 (Gym Sauce ×3): × 1.30 (+30%)
Bracket 4 (Boss Buster ×2): × 1.30 (+30%)
Bracket 5 (Speedy Boi): × 1.40 (+40%)
Total Damage: 236.6 = 2.37× multiplier

❌ Scenario B: All in One Bracket

Base Damage: 100
Bracket 4 only (+100% total): × 2.00 (+100% additive)
Total Damage: 200 = 2.0× multiplier
📊 Result: Same +100% bonus total

Scenario A spreads +100% across 3 brackets → 2.37× damage
Scenario B dumps +100% in one bracket → 2.0× damage
That's 18.5% more damage just from bracket diversity! The gap gets even wider as you stack more bonuses.

🧠 Build Optimization Tips

1. Prioritize Solo-Bracket Items

Items that sit alone in their own bracket (like Speedy Boi, Giant Fork, Corrupted Sword) are extremely efficient because their bonus multiplies independently with everything else in your build. Even a small bonus in a solo bracket can outperform a larger bonus in the crowded main bracket.

2. Don't Over-Invest in Bracket 4

The Main "Damage %" bracket already has 11 items competing. Each new item you add there only contributes additively alongside all your existing bonuses. Instead, look for items in Brackets 1, 3, 5, or 8 to create multiplicative synergy.

3. Damage Tome Is Always Multiplicative

Your Damage Tome choice is critical because it's always in its own bracket. A tome with a strong damage multiplier effectively multiplies ALL your other item bonuses. Choose your tome wisely — see our Best Tomes guide.

4. Elite Damage Is a Separate Multiplier

For boss-focused builds, remember that the Elite Damage bracket (Chaos Tome, Dicehead passive, Shrines) multiplies after all other brackets. This makes it especially powerful as a final multiplier on top of an already optimized build.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What are Damage Brackets in Megabonk?

Damage Brackets are groups of damage bonuses that interact differently. Items inside the same bracket combine additively (add together), while different brackets multiply with each other. This means spreading bonuses across multiple brackets yields significantly more damage than stacking them all in one.

How many Damage Brackets are there?

There are 8 known Damage Brackets: Damage Tome, Noelle's Passive, Flat Damage Upgrades (Gym Sauce / Brass Knuckles / Idle Juice), Main Damage % (11 items), Speedy Boi, Elite Damage (Chaos Tome / Dicehead / Shrines), Corrupted Sword, and Giant Fork Megacrit.

Why is spreading damage across brackets better?

Because brackets multiply together. Having +50% in Bracket A and +50% in Bracket B gives 1.5 × 1.5 = 2.25× total damage. But stacking +100% in just one bracket gives only 1 + 1.0 = 2.0× total damage. The multiplicative effect always outperforms pure additive stacking.

How do crits interact with Damage Brackets?

Critical hits are applied after the full bracket calculation. The formula multiplies your final bracket-computed damage by (Number of Crits × Crit Damage Multiplier). This means crit builds benefit enormously from having strong bracket diversity underneath.

Does Boss Buster stack with Tactical Glasses?

Yes, but only additively — they both sit in the same Main "Damage %" bracket (Bracket 4). If you have Boss Buster (+15%) and Tactical Glasses (+20%), that bracket's total is +35%, not 1.15 × 1.20. To get multiplicative synergy, pair them with items from different brackets.
