🎯 Build Overview

Sir Chadwell scales best when you stack Size first to stabilize clear, then push Damage to hit elite/boss thresholds; add XP/Luck mid when safe, and fold in Quantity later. Bananarang sweeps lanes, Flamewalker carpets the ground, and Dexecutioner handles executes.

📈 Upgrade Path Timeline

Follow this minute-by-minute plan; high-rarity picks can preempt

0–3 min

Stabilize

Grab Size Tome and one core for reliable clear (Bananarang / Flamewalker). Take Moldy Cheese on sight. Add Turbo Socks if positioning feels rough.

3–6 min

Online

Add Dexecutioner and begin stacking Damage. If comfy, weave in XP/Luck; otherwise push Size/Damage to key ranks first.

6–10 min

Aligned Scaling

Start Quantity. Keep total weapons at 3–4 to avoid dilution. Slot Gym Sauce for a flat damage bump.

10–15 min

Spike

Clear and execute in tandem. Focus elites and push bosses to snowball lead.

15+ min

Close Out

Cap remaining Damage/Quantity. Add XP/Luck late for maximum scaling.

⚠️ Common Mistakes

❌ Rushing Quantity

Hit Size/Damage breakpoints first; add Quantity later for better returns.

❌ Too many weapons

Keep 3–4 total to avoid upgrade-pool dilution and slower scaling.

❌ Ignoring mobility

Without Turbo Socks, orb pickup和kiting更危险 —— 你的滚雪球会被拖慢。

❓ FAQ

When should I add Aegis?

Whenever you need breathing room to stack Size/Damage — especially in high-density or spiky lobbies.

Where does the Sword-centric variant excel?

Open, high-density maps where massive area and multiple hits can shine.

Do I still want XP/Luck?

Yes, but add them after your Size/Damage foundation is set so early damage checks don't fail.
