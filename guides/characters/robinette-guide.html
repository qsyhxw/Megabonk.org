💰 ROBINETTE 💰

The Economy Master - Wealth Through Strategy

S-TIER Character Best for Farming
Overview Stonks Passive Builds Strategy Pro Tips Unlock Guide FAQ

📊 Character Overview

💰 Unlock Method

Complete Forest Tier 2, then purchase with Silver

🎯 Playstyle

Economy/Gold Scaling

⚔️ Starting Weapon

Bow (Unlocked together)

📈 Difficulty

Medium - Strategic planning required

🌟 Why S-Tier? Robinette is considered one of the best characters in MegaBonk. According to the game's developer, 8 of the top 10 world record performances use Robinette. The character's ability to convert gold into both economic advantage and combat power creates a uniquely powerful scaling curve.

✅ Strengths

  • Dual scaling: Gold AND Damage from same stat
  • Consistent power growth through gold generation
  • Excellent shop value and item access
  • Less RNG-dependent than other characters
  • Dominant in long runs with exponential scaling
  • Best character for item collection and farming
  • Strong synergy with economic items

❌ Weaknesses

  • Slower early game before gold accumulates
  • Requires strategic gold management
  • Must balance spending vs holding gold for damage
  • Weaker if you don't reach shops frequently
  • Gold items are mandatory picks
  • Risk of becoming "too strong" (potential nerfs)

💎 Passive Ability: STONKS

📈 STONKS - Dual Scaling Power

Robinette's unique passive ability provides two powerful effects that work together to create exponential scaling:

  • Gold Generation Scaling: +1% Gold Gain per character level (at level 50, you gain 50% more gold from all sources)
  • Damage Scaling: +10% Damage per 1,000 gold in your inventory (10,000 gold = +100% damage = double damage)

How It Works:

  • Level 1: +1% gold gain, minimal damage boost
  • Level 20 with 5,000 gold: +20% gold gain, +50% damage
  • Level 50 with 15,000 gold: +50% gold gain, +150% damage (2.5x damage)
  • Late game (Level 70+ with 30,000+ gold): +70% gold gain, +300% damage (4x damage)
💡 Core Strategy: The Stonks passive creates a critical decision: Do you spend gold to buy powerful items, or hold gold for massive damage scaling? The optimal strategy is to build strong gold generation early, then hold onto wealth in the late game when you've already purchased key items. This converts your accumulated wealth directly into combat power.
⚠️ Gold Management: Unlike other characters, Robinette must carefully manage gold spending. Every purchase temporarily reduces your damage! Early game, spend freely to build your economy. Mid-game, be selective. Late game with 20,000+ gold, only buy S-tier items unless absolutely necessary.

🔄 Passive Synergy Examples

  • Piggy Bank + Stonks: Piggy Bank generates 1 gold per second. With 50% gold gain bonus, that's 1.5 gold/second passively building both your wallet and damage
  • Greed + Stonks: Greed increases gold drops by 10%. Combined with Stonks' scaling, you reach critical gold mass faster
  • Crown + Stonks: Crown gives gold per wave completed. The longer you survive, the faster your damage scales
  • Lucky Coin + Stonks: More bonus gold drops = faster damage scaling throughout the run

🎯 Recommended Builds

1. 💎 Maximum Gold Scaling (S-Tier Meta)

Goal: Stack gold generation, then leverage massive damage from held gold

Core Items (Priority Order):

  1. Piggy Bank - Core passive gold generation
  2. Greed - Increases all gold sources by 10%
  3. Lucky Coin - Bonus gold drop chances
  4. Crown - Scaling gold per wave (exponential late game)
  5. Metal Detector - Find hidden gold sources

Supporting Stats:

  • Pickup Range - Collect gold efficiently
  • Luck - Better shop rolls and gold procs
  • Max HP - Survive to leverage gold scaling
  • Regeneration - Sustain without spending gold on healing

Gameplan:

Focus entirely on gold generation for the first 10-15 minutes. Buy every gold item you see. Once you have 3-4 gold items generating 200+ gold per minute, STOP SPENDING. Your damage will scale automatically from accumulated wealth. By 20+ minutes with 20,000+ gold, you'll have massive damage without needing traditional combat items.

💰 Gold Threshold Strategy:
  • 0-5,000 gold: Spend freely on economy and survival
  • 5,000-15,000 gold: Be selective, only buy excellent items
  • 15,000+ gold: Hoard wealth, damage is already scaling well
  • 25,000+ gold: Only buy absolutely broken items (Kevin, Mirror)

2. ⚡ Hybrid Economy Fighter

Goal: Balance gold generation with direct combat scaling

Core Items (Priority Order):

  1. Piggy Bank OR Greed - Choose one economy foundation
  2. Strong Weapon - Kevin, Dexecutioner, or similar high-DPS option
  3. Attack Speed Items - Amplify weapon effectiveness
  4. Lucky Coin - Passive gold generation
  5. Damage/Crit Items - Stack multiplicative damage

Supporting Stats:

  • Damage % - Multiply both weapon and Stonks damage
  • Attack Speed - Weapon DPS scaling
  • Crit Chance/Damage - Burst damage spikes

Gameplan:

Don't go all-in on economy. Pick 1-2 gold items early for the Stonks scaling boost, then focus on traditional damage items. Use your gold advantage to buy weapons and combat items that other characters can't afford. This build is more forgiving and less dependent on perfect gold management.

3. 🛍️ Shop Optimization Master

Goal: Maximize shop interactions to find perfect items

Core Items (Priority Order):

  1. Greed + Crown - Maximum gold income
  2. Luck Tome + Lucky Coin - Better shop quality
  3. Any Gold Generation Items - Fund shop purchases
  4. Anvil - Reroll items for better options
  5. Meta S-Tier Items - Kevin, Mirror, Time Warp

Supporting Stats:

  • Luck - Critical for shop quality
  • XP Gain - More levels = more shop encounters
  • Gold Generation - Afford multiple purchases

Gameplan:

Prioritize reaching shops and treasure chests. Save gold specifically for important purchases. With high luck, shops will offer better items on average. Use Anvil to reroll bad options. This strategy finds the best possible item combinations but requires patience and strategic planning.

⏱️ Strategy Timeline

0-5 Minutes: Foundation Phase

Goal: Survive early waves and establish economy base

  • Focus on basic survival - don't be greedy with risky plays
  • Pick up ALL gold drops (pickup range is valuable)
  • Take any weapon that helps you clear waves safely
  • Buy your first gold generation item ASAP
  • Stonks passive is weak early - play conservatively
💡 Early Game Tip: You only have 1% gold gain per level early, so Stonks doesn't help much yet. Your damage scaling from held gold is also minimal with only 500-1,000 gold. Play like a normal character and focus on survival.

5-15 Minutes: Economy Establishment

Goal: Build gold engine and start scaling

  • Buy Piggy Bank or Greed in first shop
  • Stack 2-3 total gold generation items
  • Your gold gain bonus is now 15-25% - noticeable improvement
  • With 3,000-5,000 gold, you're gaining +30-50% damage
  • Balance economy items with one good weapon
  • Visit every shop and treasure chest possible
💰 Gold Benchmarks:
  • 5 minutes: Target 2,000-3,000 gold
  • 10 minutes: Target 5,000-7,000 gold
  • 15 minutes: Target 8,000-12,000 gold
If you're hitting these benchmarks, you're scaling well!

15-30 Minutes: Power Spike

Goal: Leverage wealth for dominance

  • You should have 10,000-20,000 gold by now
  • That's +100-200% damage from Stonks alone!
  • Your gold gain is 30-50% higher than base
  • Start being SELECTIVE with purchases
  • Only buy S-tier items or critical survival needs
  • Your held gold IS your damage - don't waste it on mediocre items
⚠️ Critical Decision Point: This is where Robinette players make or break their run. Do NOT buy every item you see. Each 1,000 gold spent is -10% damage. Only purchase items that give more value than the damage you lose by spending.

30+ Minutes: Economic Domination

Goal: Become unstoppable

  • 20,000-40,000+ gold in the bank
  • +200-400% damage from Stonks passive
  • Gold generation is exponential with Crown scaling
  • You're practically invincible with proper items
  • Only spend gold on absolutely broken items
  • Consider completing item collections for achievements
🏆 Late Game Optimization: With 30,000+ gold, you have 4x base damage before counting any other items. At this point, gold generation continues to accelerate. You can afford to buy 1-2 expensive items per shop if they're truly excellent, but your wealth hoard is your true power source.

💡 Pro Tips & Advanced Strategies

✅ Advanced Techniques

  • Damage Math: Always calculate if buying an item is worth it. A 1,500 gold item costs you 15% damage. Does it give more than 15% damage value? If not, skip it
  • Pickup Range Priority: More range = faster gold collection = faster damage scaling. This is one of the most underrated stats for Robinette
  • Luck Investment: Luck improves shop quality AND Lucky Coin proc rates. Very valuable for economy builds
  • Crown Timing: Crown gets better every wave. In 30+ minute runs, Crown becomes your best gold item
  • Shop Route Planning: Plan movement to hit shops right after big gold waves for maximum purchasing power
  • Selective Rerolling: Don't reroll randomly. Have specific items you're looking for, then reroll until you find them
  • Gold Floor Strategy: Maintain minimum 10,000-15,000 gold in late game for consistent +100-150% damage baseline
  • Shrine Priority: Greed Shrines (exchange HP for gold) are excellent for Robinette since gold = damage
  • Defensive Scaling: Regeneration and Max HP allow you to avoid buying healing items, preserving gold for damage
  • Combo Recognition: Learn which item combos are worth breaking your gold hoard for (Kevin + Attack Speed, Mirror + Any Weapon)

❌ Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Buying Every Item: The biggest mistake! Late game, only buy truly excellent items. Your gold IS your damage
  • Over-investing in Economy: 5+ gold items is overkill. You'll generate gold too fast to spend efficiently
  • Ignoring Survival: Gold means nothing if you die. Buy HP/Regen when truly needed
  • Not Tracking Gold Benchmarks: If you're far below target gold amounts, you're not building economy correctly
  • Spending in Late Game: With 20,000+ gold and +200% damage, don't buy mediocre items just because you can afford them
  • Skipping Pickup Range: Slow gold collection directly hurts your damage scaling speed
  • Random Rerolling: Rerolls cost gold. Only reroll with specific target items in mind
  • Neglecting Luck: Luck affects shop quality significantly. Worth investing in
  • Taking Early Damage Items: Let Stonks handle damage. Focus economy first, damage second
  • Fighting Without Weapons: While Stonks gives damage, you still need a weapon to apply it. Don't skip all combat items
⚠️ Critical Mistake: The most common error is treating Robinette like other characters and spending all gold immediately. Remember: 1,000 gold held = +10% damage. That's a permanent damage item that costs nothing! Manage your wealth strategically.

🎯 Decision Framework: Should I Buy This Item?

Ask yourself these questions:

  1. How much does it cost? (Cost ÷ 1,000 = damage % you'll lose)
  2. What does it give? (Does it give more value than the damage loss?)
  3. Is it S-tier? (Only S-tier items are worth big purchases late game)
  4. Do I need it to survive? (HP/Regen when low = always worth it)
  5. Does it complete a combo? (Kevin + Attack Speed = worth breaking the bank)

Example: Item costs 3,000 gold (= -30% damage). Does it give +30% value? If yes, buy. If no, skip.

🔗 Best Item Synergies for Stonks

💰 Core Gold Generation Combos

  • Piggy Bank + Greed: Multiplicative gold generation - generates 1 gold/second, increased by 10%
  • Crown + Long Runs: Crown scales exponentially. In 40+ minute runs, Crown generates more gold than anything
  • Lucky Coin + Luck Tome: More luck = more bonus gold procs = faster wealth accumulation
  • Metal Detector + Pickup Range: Find and collect hidden gold sources automatically
  • All Gold Items + Stonks Passive: Each item benefits from +X% gold gain per level, compounding returns

💎 Gold-to-Power Conversion Strategies

  • Large Gold Hoard + Any Weapon: 20,000 gold = +200% damage to all weapons
  • Stonks + Damage % Items: Damage % multiplies your Stonks bonus (works multiplicatively!)
  • Stonks + Crit Build: Your boosted base damage makes crits even more powerful
  • Gold Items + Kevin: Build wealth, then buy Kevin late. It scales with your massive Stonks damage
  • Stonks + Mirror: Mirror duplicates effects - all benefit from your damage scaling

📊 Stat Priority for Robinette

  1. Gold Generation - Core identity, 2-4 items optimal
  2. Pickup Range - Essential for efficient gold collection
  3. Luck - Improves shop quality and gold proc rates
  4. Damage % - Multiplies your Stonks scaling
  5. Max HP/Regen - Survival without spending gold on healing
  6. Attack Speed - If using weapon-focused build
  7. XP Gain - More levels = better Stonks scaling + more shops

🚫 Items to Generally Avoid

  • Expensive Mediocre Items: If it costs 2,000+ gold and isn't S-tier, skip it
  • Duplicate Economy Items: 5+ gold items generates more than you can spend
  • Pure Survival Items in Late Game: Your damage should keep you safe. Only buy if truly struggling
  • Reroll Spam: Each reroll costs gold (damage). Be selective

🔓 How to Unlock Robinette

Unlock Requirements

  1. Complete Forest Tier 2
  2. Purchase Robinette from Unlocks menu using Silver currency
  3. Bow weapon is unlocked simultaneously

Step 1: Reach Forest Biome

Select Forest from the biome menu. This is one of the starting biomes available to all players.

Step 2: Complete Forest Tier 1

Successfully beat Forest Tier 1 to unlock Tier 2 access. Practice fundamentals here before attempting higher difficulties.

  • Learn enemy patterns and spawn locations
  • Practice movement and positioning
  • Test different character builds

Step 3: Complete Forest Tier 2

Beat Forest Tier 2 to unlock Robinette for purchase. This tier has significantly tougher enemies.

  • Use a strong character like MegaChad, Fox, or Dicehead
  • Focus on damage scaling and survival items
  • Prioritize S-tier items from shops and chests
  • Learn boss patterns to avoid unnecessary damage
  • Build HP/Regen for safety margin

Step 4: Purchase with Silver

After completing Tier 2, go to the Unlocks menu and purchase Robinette using Silver earned from runs. The character and Bow weapon unlock together.

💡 Unlock Tips: Forest Tier 2 is moderately challenging. If struggling:
  • Farm Silver on Tier 1 to buy permanent upgrades first
  • Learn optimal builds from other character guides
  • Practice positioning and dodging enemy attacks
  • Focus on damage + survival balance, not pure offense

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Is Robinette actually S-tier or is it exaggerated?

Robinette is legitimately S-tier. According to MegaBonk's developer, 8 of the top 10 world record performances use Robinette. The character's dual scaling (gold generation + damage from held gold) creates uniquely powerful exponential growth. However, the developer has considered nerfs due to Robinette's dominance.

How many gold items should I stack?

Optimal is 2-4 gold generation items. 2 is minimum for economy advantage, 3-4 is the sweet spot for wealth without overkill. 5+ gold items generates more wealth than you can efficiently spend, and you'd be better served buying combat items instead.

Should I hoard gold or spend it?

Both! Early game (0-10 min), spend freely to build economy and survival. Mid game (10-20 min), be selective and start accumulating. Late game (20+ min), hoard wealth - your damage comes from held gold. Only spend on S-tier items or critical survival needs.

What's the best first shop purchase?

Priority: Piggy Bank > Greed > Any Gold Item > Strong Weapon > Survival Items. Get your economy engine started first, then add damage/survival as needed.

Does the Stonks damage bonus work with all attacks?

Yes! The +10% damage per 1,000 gold applies to all damage sources - weapons, abilities, and even item effects. It's a universal damage multiplier based on your wealth.

What happens if I spend all my gold?

Your Stonks damage bonus decreases! Every 1,000 gold you spend reduces your damage by 10%. This is why late-game gold management is critical. Only spend on truly valuable items.

Does Robinette work on all biomes?

Yes! The economy strategy works everywhere. However, biomes with more shops and treasure chests (Desert, Factory) give slight advantages. Forest is also excellent. The character's power comes from the Stonks passive, not just shops.

Can I complete all achievements with Robinette?

Absolutely! Robinette is excellent for most achievements. The character excels at "Collect X Items" challenges (buy everything), "Complete Long Runs" (scales forever), and "High Damage" challenges (Stonks scaling). Speed challenges are slightly harder since economy builds take time to scale.

Why might Robinette get nerfed?

The developer noted that Robinette dominates leaderboards with 8/10 top performances. The character's dual scaling (gold generation AND damage) plus exponential growth in long runs makes it statistically too strong. However, the developer also said "I don't really wanna nerf her because it's fun af" so nerfs may never happen.

Is the damage scaling capped?

No known cap! You can accumulate 50,000+ gold in very long runs, giving +500% damage (6x multiplier). Combined with gold generation bonuses that also scale with levels, Robinette has theoretically infinite scaling potential.

What's the world record strategy?

Top players focus on maximum gold generation early (4 gold items by 15 minutes), then never spend gold after reaching 15,000-20,000. They leverage the massive damage bonus to survive indefinitely, only buying critical items that significantly improve survivability. The goal is to scale damage to 400-500% (40,000-50,000 gold) while maintaining perfect survival items.
