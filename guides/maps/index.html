Overview

Megabonk currently features 2 maps ‚Äî Forest and Desert ‚Äî each with 3 difficulty tiers. Unlike traditional roguelikes with dozens of standalone stages, Megabonk uses a tier progression system where the number of stages increases with difficulty. Tier 1 has 1 stage, Tier 2 has 2 stages, and Tier 3 features all 3 stages culminating in an intense final boss encounter.

Each map offers distinct terrain, enemy types, and environmental mechanics that dramatically affect your build strategy. Forest provides natural cover and verticality, while Desert challenges you with open terrain and visibility-reducing sandstorms. Understanding these differences is crucial for optimizing your character builds and reaching the leaderboards.

üí° Pro Tip: The tier system determines both difficulty and progression. You must complete each tier before unlocking the next, and higher tiers require defeating bosses to advance through portals between stages.

How Stage Progression Works

After defeating a stage boss, a portal appears that transports you to the next stage. However, there are important restrictions:

  • Tier 1: No portal appears after the boss. Run ends after defeating the boss and surviving the final swarm.
  • Tier 2: Portal opens after Stage 1 boss, leading to Stage 2. Run ends after Stage 2 boss.
  • Tier 3: Portals open after both Stage 1 and Stage 2 bosses, leading to the ultimate final boss encounter.
‚ö†Ô∏è Important: You cannot skip tiers. To access Tier 3, you must first complete Tier 1 and Tier 2. Each tier must be selected manually from the map screen before starting a run.

How to Unlock Desert Map

Desert is locked by default and requires specific steps to unlock. You must reach Stage 2 of Forest Tier 2 while playing as CL4NK.

Step-by-Step Unlock Guide:

  1. Unlock CL4NK Character
    Kill 7,500 total enemies across all runs to unlock the Revolver weapon, then purchase CL4NK from the shop using silver coins.
  2. Complete Forest Tier 1
    Play any character through Forest Tier 1, defeat the boss (Lil Bark), and survive the final swarm to unlock Tier 2.
  3. Start Forest Tier 2 as CL4NK
    Select Forest Tier 2 from the map screen and choose CL4NK as your character.
  4. Defeat Stage 1 Boss
    Build your character through Stage 1, defeat Chadbark, and enter the portal that appears.
  5. Enter Stage 2 Portal
    As soon as you step through the portal into Stage 2, Desert map unlocks. You don't need to complete Stage 2 ‚Äî just reaching it is enough.
üí° Farming Tip: The 7,500 enemy requirement for CL4NK can be tracked in your stats. Play Tier 1 repeatedly with fast-clearing characters like Fox or Robinette to accumulate kills quickly.

Hidden Bosses

Both maps contain optional hidden boss encounters that unlock new characters:

  • Bush (Forest) - Interact with Suspicious Bush statue to trigger. Defeating unlocks Bush character. Requires Sniper Rifle unlocked.
  • Bandit (Desert) - Interact with Bandit statue. Defeat while carrying Dexecutioner weapon to unlock Bandit character.

Map-Specific Strategies

Forest Tactics

Forest's terrain heavily favors defensive and positioning-based strategies:

  • Use vertical terrain: Jump mechanics shine here. Extra jump height and jump count provide enormous tactical advantages for dodging and exploring.
  • Hide behind obstacles: Trees and rocks break line of sight with ranged enemies. Use them to recover HP and reposition.
  • Corner enemies: Natural valleys and hills allow you to funnel enemy groups into chokepoints for AoE weapons.
  • Scout carefully: Shrines can hide behind trees. Explore systematically to avoid missing charge shrines.
Best Forest Characters: Fox (luck scaling), Robinette (gold/damage scaling), and Birdo (natural jump bonus + gliding) excel in Forest due to mobility and sustained damage capabilities.

Desert Survival Guide

Desert demands aggressive builds with high burst damage and mobility:

  • Prioritize movement speed: Open terrain means enemies can swarm from all directions. Speed is survival.
  • Manage Skeleton Balls: These rolling enemies spawn early and can block shrine access. Invest in damage or evasion to handle them.
  • Use tornadoes strategically: Desert tornadoes provide massive vertical lift. Use them to escape ground swarms and survey the map.
  • Sandstorm awareness: When sandstorms hit, avoid exploring new areas. Stay in cleared zones until visibility returns.
  • Avoid Bandit statue: Unless specifically attempting the unlock, skip the Bandit fight. It's extremely difficult and adds unnecessary risk.
Desert Difficulty Spike: Players consistently report Desert being significantly harder than Forest. Don't attempt Desert Tier 3 until you've comfortably cleared Forest Tier 3 multiple times. The open terrain and aggressive enemy spawns require optimized builds.

Shrines & Environmental Mechanics

Every stage contains exactly 15 Charge Shrines (unless you pick up the Beacon item, which adds more). Charging shrines provides random stat upgrades crucial for late-game scaling.

Shrine Timing Strategy

Enemy spawns follow predictable patterns:

  • 3:00 - First miniboss spawns
  • 4:00 - Enemy swarm event
  • 7:00 - Second enemy swarm
  • 8:00 - Second miniboss spawns
  • 10:00 - Final boss portal opens / final swarm begins
üí° Shrine Charging Tip: Avoid charging shrines during miniboss spawns (3:00, 8:00) or swarms (4:00, 7:00). Getting knocked out of the charging zone fails the charge and wastes time. Clear enemies first, then charge in safety.

Desert-Exclusive: Sandstorms

Desert stages feature periodic sandstorms that drastically reduce visibility. During sandstorms:

  • Vision range drops to near-zero
  • Enemies are only visible when very close
  • Navigation becomes extremely difficult
  • Charging a shrine during a sandstorm counts toward the Tornado weapon unlock quest

Desert guarantees at least one sandstorm per run. Prepare by clearing enemy clusters before sandstorms hit and avoid exploration during reduced visibility.

Beating Tier 3: Expert Tips

Tier 3 represents Megabonk's ultimate challenge. Success requires build optimization, shrine mastery, and perfect execution across all 3 stages.

Essential Tier 3 Strategy

The Holy Trinity: Prioritize XP Tome + Luck Tome + Cursed Tome early in Stage 1. These three tomes create exponential scaling that carries through all stages. XP speeds leveling, Luck improves upgrade quality, and Cursed increases drop rarity despite the difficulty spike.

Build Preparation Checklist

  • ‚úÖ Unlock all weapon/tome slots (4 weapons, 4 tomes minimum)
  • ‚úÖ Unlock Banish ability (complete 40 quests) - removes bad items from pool
  • ‚úÖ Unlock Toggler (buy 40 items) - disables unwanted items
  • ‚úÖ Disable bad items: Borgar, Speed Boi, and situational items that don't fit your build
  • ‚úÖ Raise Difficulty to ~40% before leaving Stage 1 for increased XP gain

Stage-by-Stage Focus

Stage 1 (Forest): Farm XP aggressively. Get XP Tome, Luck Tome, and one damage-scaling weapon. Reach level 40-50 before taking boss portal. Don't rush - the stronger you leave Stage 1, the easier Stage 2 and 3 become.

Stage 2 (Desert): Complete your weapon loadout. Add Aegis for survivability and Dexecutioner for execute damage. Scale your best weapon heavily with Quantity/Projectile tomes. Target level 70-80 before final boss.

Stage 3 (Final Boss): Focus survives over DPS. HP Regen becomes critical since Lifesteal won't work during boss shield phases. Keep moving, destroy pylons immediately, and play defensively until you get weapons back.

‚ö†Ô∏è Defense is Mandatory: Both Tier 3 bosses have mechanics that make pure DPS builds fail. You MUST have HP Regen (from tomes) or Aegis (weapon shield) to survive pylon phases. Many players fail Tier 3 by over-investing in damage and ignoring survivability.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many maps are in Megabonk?

Megabonk currently has 2 maps: Forest and Desert. Each map has 3 tiers with increasing difficulty levels.

How do I unlock the Desert map?

To unlock Desert, you must: 1) Unlock CL4NK by killing 7,500 total enemies and obtaining the Revolver, 2) Play Forest Tier 2 as CL4NK, 3) Defeat the first boss and enter the Stage 2 portal. Desert unlocks automatically when you reach Stage 2.

What is the difference between Tiers and Stages?

Tiers determine difficulty level and how many stages you'll play. Tier 1 has 1 stage, Tier 2 has 2 stages, and Tier 3 has 3 stages. Each stage ends with a boss fight.

Which map is harder, Forest or Desert?

Desert is significantly more difficult than Forest. It features more aggressive enemies, open terrain with less cover, sandstorm effects that reduce visibility, and tougher boss mechanics. Most players recommend mastering Forest Tier 3 before attempting Desert Tier 2.

Can I skip directly to Tier 3?

No. You must complete Tier 1 to unlock Tier 2, and complete Tier 2 to unlock Tier 3. Each tier must be selected manually from the map screen before starting a new run.

Do higher tiers unlock new maps?

No. Higher tiers don't introduce new maps but instead increase the number of stages you progress through using the existing Forest and Desert maps. Tier 3 takes you through Forest, then Desert, then a final boss arena.

What happens after I defeat a stage boss?

In Tier 2 and 3, a portal appears after defeating a stage boss. Enter the portal to advance to the next stage. In Tier 1, no portal appears and the run ends after the boss and final swarm.

How many Shrines are on each map?

Each stage has exactly 15 Charge Shrines by default. This number increases if you pick up the Beacon item, which adds 2 additional shrines per stage. For Chaos Tome unlock attempts, banish Beacon to keep the count at 15.
