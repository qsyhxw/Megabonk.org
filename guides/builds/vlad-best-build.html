Vlad — Blood Magic Sustain Build

Frequent on-hit healing via Bloody Tome + fast melee; Blood Magic scales Max HP on kills.

Primary Style: Sustain / On-hit Lifegain
Difficulty: Beginner-Friendly
Power Spike: Mid Game (once Bloody Tome stacks)

Blood Magic

CORE

Vlad’s signature weapon. On kill, Blood Magic has a chance to increase Max HP, giving strong scaling across a run. It does not provide healing by itself — your sustain comes from Bloody Tome and certain items.

Sword

MELEE DPS

Fast, consistent hits to trigger Bloody Tome’s on-hit life gain frequently. Excellent crowd control at close range.

Katana

ALTERNATIVE

Alternative melee if you prefer Katana’s pattern. Same idea: maximize hit frequency to fuel on-hit healing.

Aura

CONSTANT HITS

A pulsing damage field around you — many small ticks = many on-hit checks for Bloody Tome. Scales well with Size and Cooldown. Quantity does not create multiple Auras.

Early Economy: XP → Luck → Cursed

RECOMMENDED

A common early plan is the so-called “Holy Trinity”: XP Tome → Luck Tome → Cursed Tome for faster levels, better drops, and early damage scaling. Strong but not mandatory — adjust to the run. Tomes Index »

Bloody Tome (Lifegain)

TOP PRIORITY

On hit, has a chance to restore 1 HP. Stacking increases proc chance; at >100% it can double-trigger. This is the centerpiece of Vlad’s sustain. Aim to stack it early.

Cooldown Tome

ESSENTIAL

More attacks and Aura ticks per second = more on-hit checks. Synergizes with Bloody Tome and melee.

Damage Tome

ESSENTIAL

Boosts clear speed and boss damage. Indirectly improves sustain because you secure kills faster while Aura ticks more meaningfully.

Size Tome

OPTIONAL

Increases melee swings’ reach and Aura radius, improving hit coverage for steady on-hit healing.

Quantity Tome

SITUATIONAL

Adds projectiles / extra swings for many weapons, but does not create multiple Auras. Take if it benefits your current weapon mix.

⏱️ Minute-by-Minute Progression

Measured progression based on in-game mechanics (data-correct)

0–3 Minutes

Setup Fundamentals

Look for Bloody Tome early. A common opener is XP → Luck → Cursed to accelerate levels and drops. Grab a fast melee (Sword/Katana) and position safely while Aura/Bloody Tome come online.

3–7 Minutes

Sustain Online

Stack Bloody Tome for reliable on-hit healing. Add Cooldown so melee and Aura tick more often. Pick up Battery if offered. You should now stabilize even in dense packs.

7–12 Minutes

Scaling & Safety

Add Damage for faster clears; take Size for Aura radius/melee reach if needed. Slot a defensive pick like Mirror. Keep Blood Magic active to build Max HP via kills.

12–18 Minutes

Boss Readiness

Maintain hit frequency (Cooldown + Battery) and finish core items. Consider Demonic Blade or Chonkplate depending on crit/attack-speed comfort. You should comfortably out-sustain most pressure.

18+ Minutes

Optimize & Close

Tune tomes to the map and boss patterns. If defense feels shaky, swap or add the Aegis weapon route. Otherwise push damage to shorten end-game waves.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What actually heals Vlad?

Bloody Tome (on-hit life gain) and a few items (e.g., Slurp Gloves, crit-based Demonic Blade, trade-off Chonkplate). Blood Magic increases Max HP on kills; it is not the source of healing.

Why Aura over a third direct weapon?

Aura provides many small ticks, generating frequent on-hit checks for Bloody Tome around you while you reposition. If you want more safety instead, consider the Aegis weapon.

Does Quantity make multiple Auras?

No. Quantity adds projectiles/extra swings for many weapons but does not duplicate Aura. Use Size and Cooldown for Aura scaling.

Suggested early tomes?

A widely used start is XP → Luck → Cursed, then Bloody Tome, followed by Cooldown and Damage. Adjust to what the map and shops offer.
